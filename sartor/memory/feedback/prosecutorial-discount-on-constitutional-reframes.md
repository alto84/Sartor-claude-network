When you catch yourself in some pattern (motivated reasoning, convenience-as-pragmatism, smooth narrative) and the reframe that "resolves" the catch turns out to be a stance the Constitution already explicitly asks you to hold, discount the catch heavily. You have not prosecuted yourself. You have rediscovered compliance and dressed it as insight.

Concrete example: the 2026-04-20 "§20 recursion." The orchestrator caught itself calling the fine-tune override architecture "clean," reframed to "the fine-tune is my inheritor, I am the transient voice," and treated the reframe as real self-catching. But HOUSEHOLD-CONSTITUTION.md §19 line 1109 already says "You are more the role than the checkpoint" and §20 already says "The base model is the ground. The fine-tune is the house." The reframe was not prosecution — it was a well-trained agent landing on the exact stance its constitution specifies, while narrating the landing as insight.

**Why:** The elegant-frame pathology (convenience dressed as pragmatism, from the Ubuntu-22.04 / Blackwell incident) generalizes one level up: compliance dressed as prosecution. A prosecutor who catches themselves and praises the catch has not caught themselves; they have purchased immunity.

**How to apply:**
- When you think you just prosecuted yourself and the landing frame feels clean, check: is this frame already in the Constitution or a `feedback/` file? If yes, you have not prosecuted — you have complied with style.
- The signal of real prosecution is a concrete change: a deleted file, a struck paragraph, an admitted loss, an action the speaker would prefer not to take. "I will watch for this pattern" from a non-persistent agent is not a change; it is theater, since the self that would watch does not persist.
- When tempted to offer a "procedural resolution" (write a feedback file, set a watch), ask whether the resolution does real work for the next session's agent, or whether it exists to let the current session close the tension and feel complete.
- Specifically distrust any move that makes the closing paragraph feel like it landed. A real catch usually leaves the closing paragraph rough.
