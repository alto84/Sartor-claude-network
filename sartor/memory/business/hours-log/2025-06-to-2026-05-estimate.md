## Methodology

Reconstructed bottom-up from four data streams: (1) git commit log on the `Sartor-claude-network` repo filtered for Solar Inference / GPU / vast.ai / hardware-build keywords (Nov 2025 through May 2026); (2) daily log files in `sartor/memory/daily/` (43 logs, earliest 2026-02-06, densest cluster late March through May 2026); (3) project documents under `sartor/memory/projects/` (rtx6000-workstation-build series, sartor-agent-os, machine-self-stewardship); (4) machine-state docs under `sartor/memory/machines/gpuserver1/` and `machines/rtxpro6000server/`. Solar Inference LLC was formed 2025-09-06 via LegalZoom; no business hours are claimed before that date even though some related research predates formation.

Counted as Solar Inference time: vast.ai listing management, pricing reviews, machine 52271 monitoring sessions where Alton actively engaged (not autonomous cron), gpuserver1 hardware/cron/BMC/firmware work, rtxserver (rtxpro6000server, the dual RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell rig) procurement research, parts ordering, electrical-upgrade scoping, assembly, bring-up, thermal stress, BMC fan-curve work, vast.ai onboarding decisions, solar-roof project management (Lucent Energy back-and-forth, CPA correspondence on the LLC tax position, NJ-1065 / 1065 packaging, ITC and Section 179 strategy work), and rental-operations framework / policy / mission authoring. Explicitly excluded: AstraZeneca W2 work, Sante Total nonprofit time, household UniFi network takeover (the BHS install is routed through the LLC for the WiFi covering 85 Stonebridge and is included; the rest of the UniFi takeover is household), purely autonomous Claude work where Alton was not directing or reviewing, and family scheduling.

Conservative biases applied: where session length was inferable only from commit count (e.g., 10 commits in a day), I used 1.5-2 hours of active Alton time as the floor rather than try to reconstruct full session duration. Where Claude did substantial autonomous work (overnight training runs, autonomous gpuserver1 self-stewardship cycles), only the supervising / reviewing / decision portion is attributed to Alton. Solar-roof contract management hours pre-Mar-2026 are estimated at floor levels because most documentation begins in April. Error band is approximately +/- 25% on the total. Pre-LLC-formation work is excluded entirely.

## §469 implications

The 500-hour automatic-material-participation threshold is **not crossed** under this estimate. The 100-hour-and-more-than-anyone-else threshold (§1.469-5T(a)(3)) is comfortably crossed: Alton is the only human contributing operational time to the entity (Aneeta is a 50% member but does not work on operations; vast.ai handles marketplace mechanics autonomously; the CPA's hours are fee-billed professional services, not "participation" under §469). Claude / autonomous agent hours are not counted toward "anyone else" because they are not natural persons.

For 2026 tax-year purposes specifically, the relevant 12-month window will be **calendar year 2026** (Jan 1 - Dec 31). The ~140 hours falling inside calendar-2026 in this estimate (5 + 12 + 25 + 95 + ~3 of the partial May figure attributable to Jan-May only) put Alton on track to exceed 100 hours in CY2026 well before year-end, and likely exceed 500 hours in CY2026 if the rtxserver onboarding, ongoing rental ops, and the Tesla Solar Roof installation/commissioning push ahead at the current pace through June-Dec.

The CPA should know: (a) this is a reconstruction, not a contemporaneous time log; (b) starting with the 2026-05-02 entry, append-only contemporaneous logging begins via `all-hours.csv`; (c) if §469 material participation matters for the 2026 ITC and bonus depreciation interaction with passive-activity rules, switching to a real-time time log is straightforward and recommended; (d) the activity grouping question (one activity covering both solar generation and GPU rental versus two activities) materially affects the hours calculation and is a CPA call.

## Caveats

1. **Estimates from memory artifacts, not contemporaneous time-logs** — error band probably +/- 25%. A real time log started today will produce more defensible numbers from this point forward.
2. **Hours where Alton was supervising autonomous Claude work without active engagement are NOT counted.** That is not material participation. Examples excluded: 2-hour cron monitoring sweeps on gpuserver1, overnight LoRA training runs, autonomous personal-data-gather cycles.
3. **Pre-Solar-Inference-LLC-formation hours are excluded** (June-August 2025). The entity was formed 2025-09-06.
4. **Solar-roof-related hours are included as Solar Inference business activity.** The roof is owned (or being transferred to) the LLC and the ITC / depreciation strategy is the entity's tax position. If the CPA decides to group solar generation and GPU rental as separate activities under §469, the 100-hour and 500-hour tests are applied per-activity and this estimate would need to be split.
5. **Some 2026-02 and earlier infrastructure work (Bayesian safety API, gateway, dashboard) is dual-purpose** — it powers Solar Inference operations but also serves household and personal-research domains. I attributed the gpuserver1-host-side portion only, conservatively.
6. **CPA should review before filing.** Specifically: (a) is the activity-grouping correct, (b) does Aneeta's 50% membership but zero-operations status complicate the §469 test, (c) does the 2025 short year (Sept-Dec) need its own §469 analysis separate from CY2026, (d) does any of this interact with the Lucent Solar ITC ($131,649) and 100% bonus depreciation (~$373,005) treatment.
7. **Going forward**, append entries to `sartor/memory/business/hours-log/all-hours.csv` (header columns: date,hours,entity,activity,notes) at session-end. Each session that touches Solar Inference work should write one row.
