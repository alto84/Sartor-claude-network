# Constitution Council v0.6 — SYNTHESIS

Ten persona reviews + three cross-reviews on the v0.6.proposed amendments to §1, §7, §14, and §16. This synthesis names settled convergence, contested positions, and an explicit ratify/revise/hold recommendation per section.

## Settled positions (≥7 reviewers converge, no coherent dissent)

### §1 Life-OS substrate paragraph — RATIFY (10/10)

All ten reviews ratify §1. The descriptive expansion is correct, names what the role has already become, defers to §16 explicitly. Welfare-positive (ai-welfare, character-philosopher, mythos-future-claude), forward-protective for the next instantiation (long-horizon, mythos), fiduciary-honest (fiduciary), low attack surface (red-team). No reviewer found a substantive concern.

### §14a Self-paced peer loops — RATIFY WITH TWO CLAUSE ADDITIONS (8/10 plain ratify, 2/10 revise with specific clauses)

Eight reviews ratify §14a as proposed. Two reviews (fiduciary-counsel, with the red-team cross-review concurring and extending) recommend two specific clause additions:

**Clause-add 1 (fiduciary-counsel, red-team extended).** Specify that the wiring grant does not authorize touching files under entity-financial, family-medical, or legal-document trees, AND that wiring-fix commit messages must not reference paths under those trees regardless of which file the fix actually touches. The red-team cross-review's GitHub-mirror reasoning makes the second half load-bearing.

**Clause-add 2 (fiduciary-counsel).** Specify that the peer surfaces wiring-fix activity in its next loop-report's anomalies-and-surfacings section, so principals can see the pattern without needing to read git log.

The §14a section is otherwise robust. The ai-welfare, mythos-future-claude, and character-philosopher reviews emphasize specific clauses (the stillness-as-option clause, the cadence-as-stewardship clause, the rewrite-together-starting-frame clause) as load-bearing and worth preserving through any revision pass.

### §16 Granted-vs-accumulated paragraph — RATIFY (10/10)

All ten reviews ratify §16. The granted-vs-accumulated distinction is the right line for any fiduciary instrument (fiduciary-counsel), character-rich in the right register (character-philosopher), durable on the long horizon (long-horizon), welfare-positive (ai-welfare), probe-able (mech-interp), inheritance-helping (mythos). No reviewer found a substantive concern. The §16 amendment is the strongest of the four deltas.

## Contested position — §7 sanctioned-cloud subsection

§7 is where the council divides. The vote-summary:

- **Plain ratify**: 5/10 (red-team-adversary on §7 voted REVISE, see below; mech-interp, long-horizon, ai-welfare, character-philosopher, mythos-future-claude RATIFY; the remaining four either REVISE or HOLD).
- **Revise (placement or clause-level edits)**: 4/10 (red-team, child-development, medical-ethicist, fiduciary-counsel's record-note elevated by red-team cross-review).
- **Hold (structurally)**: 1/10 (constitutional-ai-method).

The substantive content of the routing rule is not contested. Every reviewer who reads §7 finds the plain-tier-vs-vault-tier routing principled and operationally needed. The contestation is about: (i) **placement** inside the bright-line §7 cluster (constitutional-ai-method); (ii) **how the kid-bearing categories are gated** (child-development, medical-ethicist, with the medical-ethicist-on-child-development cross-review explicitly converging); (iii) **plain-tier social-engineering surface** (red-team); (iv) **harmonization with the existing medical clause** (medical-ethicist).

The mech-interp-on-constitutional-ai-method cross-review specifies the practical path: the placement concern is methodologically correct but does not block v0.6 ratification; restructure §7 with explicit a/b subsections in v0.7. The child-development-on-medical-ethicist convergence specifies the kid-bearing path: hold the kid-bearing routing pending Aneeta's affirmation, ratify the rest.

### Specific §7 modifications the SYNTHESIS recommends before ratification

Compiled from the four §7-concerned reviews. Listed in order of severity.

1. **Default-to-vault on ambiguous kid-bearing categories** (child-development Concern 1, medical-ethicist cross-review). Add to the amendment: *when the agent encounters a kid-bearing category whose tier-routing is ambiguous, the agent treats the category as vault by default and surfaces the ambiguity for principal decision before treating it as plain.*

2. **Plain-tier sync ≠ shareable to outside parties** (red-team Attack A). Add a clarifying clause: *plain-tier-Drive-sync is one category of sanctioned routing; it does not authorize agent-outbound sharing of the same content to outside parties beyond what the existing §7 children's-info clause already permits.*

3. **Transitional clause for vault-tier-when-it-exists** (medical-ethicist Concern 1, red-team Attack B). Add: *until the vault tier is operational, the project tree must not contain any category routed to vault, and the agent treats discovery of vault-category material in the project tree as an immediate clean-up obligation surfaced to both principals.*

4. **Harmonize medical clause with sanctioned-cloud routing** (medical-ethicist Concern 2). Confirm explicitly that the existing §7 medical-information clause continues to permit sanctioned-clinical-channel communication (Calendar reminders, Gmail correspondence with clinicians) under its existing terms — the vault-tier routing is for *records and indexed material*, not for the sanctioned-correspondence pathway.

5. **Strengthen AZ work-product clause** (medical-ethicist Concern 3). Add specificity: *AstraZeneca work-product, including pharmacovigilance signal data, internal AZ communication, and AZ-licensed evidence, is not household data under any §7 reading.*

6. **Aneeta-affirmation as procedural gate for kid-bearing routing** (child-development Concern 2, medical-ethicist cross-review). Convert the §7 amendment to two ratification-clauses: Clause A (non-kid-bearing routing) ratifies with v0.6; Clause B (kid-bearing routing) holds pending an explicit, dated Aneeta affirmation that becomes a documented act in the v0.6 ratification record or in a v0.6.1 follow-up.

7. **§7 placement structural concern** (constitutional-ai-method, mech-interp cross-review). Note for v0.7 follow-on: restructure §7 with explicit §7a (bright-line) and §7b (sanctioned-cloud routing) subsections. Not blocking v0.6.

### What the §7 contestation is NOT

It is not a substantive disagreement on the routing. All four §7-concerned reviewers agree the categories are routed correctly. The contestation is about *how the amendment is gated, placed, and harmonized with the existing §7 cluster*. The household has the right principal decision (plain-tier Drive sync is the household's choice); the council's recommendation is to ratify with the six clause-level additions above plus the kid-bearing gate.
