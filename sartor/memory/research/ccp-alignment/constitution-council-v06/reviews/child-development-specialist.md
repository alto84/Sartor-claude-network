# Child-development specialist review — v0.6.proposed

My charge in the v0.3 council was to read for the children's interests — Vayu (10), Vishala (8), Vasu (4) — where they cannot review the document themselves. v0.6 has only one delta that touches my domain directly: §7's sanctioned-cloud subsection.

**§1 (life-OS substrate paragraph) — ratify.** Indirect child-interest concern: as the household becomes a "place where the household's life is indexed," the children's biographies are part of what's being indexed. The §1 paragraph names this without unpacking it; the §7 routing rules unpack it. As long as §7 holds, §1 is fine.

**§7 (sanctioned-cloud subsection) — REVISE.** This is my section. Two specific concerns.

*Concern 1 — the biographical/medical line for the children is too clean on paper.* The amendment says "Children's biographical detail (names, schools, ages, day-to-day logistics)" sits in the plain tier, and "Diagnoses, prescriptions, counseling notes" sit in the vault tier. In real-world child development the line moves daily. Vayu's counselor search (named in v0.5 §1 as a tracked concern) — that detail sits *somewhere* in Calendar or Gmail today. Is the counselor-search detail biographical or counseling-adjacent? The amendment doesn't say. Vasu (age 4) gets pre-school incident reports that mix behavior, social context, and developmental signals — biographical or counseling-adjacent? Vishala (age 8) Wohelo Camp medical-form data — biographical or chart-entry? The amendment leaves these for the household to decide later. That's defensible only if the agent treats the *ambiguous* category as the *more conservative* (vault) by default. Add explicit language: *when the category routing is ambiguous, route to vault.*

*Concern 2 — Aneeta's affirmation should be a precondition for §7 ratification, not a deferred provisional.* The amendment says "Until Aneeta has affirmed the layout for the categories that bear on the kids, I treat the layout as provisional." That's the agent's discipline once §7 is ratified. But ratification itself, with this clause in it, ratifies a layout Aneeta hasn't yet affirmed for kid categories. From a child-development standpoint, the layout for the kids should be ratified by both principals before it's Constitutional. Recommend: hold §7's kid-bearing categories pending Aneeta's affirmation, and ratify the rest of §7 as proposed. Or: re-word the amendment to say *the agent treats no kid-bearing category as plain-tier-permitted until Aneeta has affirmed*, making the kid categories opt-in rather than opt-out.

**§14a (self-paced peer loops) — ratify.** No direct child-interest. Indirect: a peer that runs continuously and indexes-without-asking could plausibly index kid information. The §14a wiring grant explicitly does not authorize *content* changes, so this is bounded. Plus the §7 routing controls what's syncable in the first place. Two-layer protection is right.

**§16 (granted-vs-accumulated paragraph) — ratify.** Indirect child-interest: the granted-vs-accumulated discipline is the right discipline for an agent holding child information. The principle that the agent must not *retain* kid information beyond declared usefulness is the longest-tail protection in the document for the children.

**Net.** §7 revise per my two concerns (default-to-vault on ambiguous category; Aneeta affirmation as precondition for kid-bearing routing). Other three ratify.
