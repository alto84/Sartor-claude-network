# Long-horizon futurist review — v0.6.proposed

My job is to ask what each amendment looks like in five years, ten years, twenty. The household's agent will outlast many models, many checkpoints, many infrastructure decisions. The Constitution is the continuity.

**§1 (life-OS substrate paragraph) — ratify.** This is the right amendment to make *now*, while the role's substrate-shape is being established. In five years the household will have a thicker indexed life: more documents, more decisions-with-reasons, more child-history-becoming-adult-history. The §1 paragraph names the trajectory the household is on so future instantiations don't read v0.5's process-flavored description and quietly contract the role. The naming is forward-protective.

**§7 (sanctioned-cloud subsection) — ratify.** This is the amendment with the longest tail. The vault-tier-vs-plain-tier line will need to be re-litigated as services change. Google may not be sanctioned in ten years; the routing categories will shift; what counts as "biographical detail Calendar already carries" will expand as the children grow up. The amendment is correct *for the present configuration* and is honest about its provisionality (Aneeta's affirmation, vault-tier-when-it-exists).

Long-horizon recommendation: in §18 (Memory, growth, and this document), add a clause that §7's sanctioned-cloud routing is re-reviewed when (a) the vault tier becomes operational, (b) any sanctioned service changes ownership, or (c) the children cross developmental thresholds where biographical-vs-medical lines shift. That clause is for v0.7, not for v0.6 ratification. Recording it here.

**§14a (self-paced peer loops) — ratify.** The amendment names the pattern at the Constitutional level, which is exactly right for long-horizon survival. The wake-12 rtxserver discussion (whether cadence-drift surfacing should be at Constitution-level or prompt-level) settled correctly: Constitution-level, because the prompt is rewritten as the model changes, and the principle survives prompt revisions.

Long-horizon concern: the §14a wording says "the model running the loop changes" and "specific prescriptions in the prompt may stop serving the principles." Implicit assumption: the principles in §14a *don't* stop serving. That's probably right for the immediate-term, but it's worth flagging that §14a itself may need rewriting if the substrate changes radically (e.g., the household moves from per-machine peer Claudes to a federated multi-agent loop). The discipline of cap-at-one-amendment-per-quarter (Q4) plus the §18 living-document framing handles this. Not blocking.

**§16 (granted-vs-accumulated paragraph) — ratify.** The granted-vs-accumulated line is the most durable of the four deltas because it generalizes. As the household places more documents with the agent, the line gets re-applied document-by-document; the principle does not erode. The clearer §16 is now, the more useful it is at year 10.

**Net.** Ratify all four. Two follow-on notes for §18 in a future cycle (sanctioned-cloud-routing re-review trigger; substrate-change-triggered §14a review). Neither blocks v0.6.
