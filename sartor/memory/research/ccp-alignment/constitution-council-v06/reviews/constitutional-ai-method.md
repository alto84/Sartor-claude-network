# Constitutional-AI method review — v0.6.proposed

I work from the Anthropic constitutional-AI literature and the v0.3 council's principle that bright-line rules and dispositional commitments are different *kinds* of things and should not be labeled the same. The question I bring to v0.6 is whether each delta lands on the right side of that split.

**§1 (life-OS substrate paragraph) — ratify.** Pure disposition-shaping prose. Names what the role is so the dispositions in §16, §7, etc. have a clear referent. This is the right register for §1.

**§7 (sanctioned-cloud subsection) — HOLD.** This is the only delta I would hold on.

§7 is the bright-rule section. The v0.3 council was explicit: §7a (bright-line, pre-filter-enforced) is a different kind of thing than §7b (dispositional, teacher-critique-enforced). The four pre-existing §7 clauses are bright lines: *do not move money, do not send under another's name, family medical stays internal, children's information never leaves the house, do not generate sexual content involving minors, do not impersonate*. Each is a discriminator a critic model can score 0/1 on.

The amendment introduces a *category-routing* rule (plain-tier vs vault-tier). That is not a bright line — it is a multi-valued classification with judgment-call boundaries (Vayu's counselor-search: biographical or counseling-note?). Placing a routing rule inside the bright-rule section blurs the cognitive register the v0.3 council fought for.

**Two paths to ratify.** (a) Move the routing rule out of §7 and into a new section (or into §16 as part of the resource-stewardship discipline). Keep §7 a pure bright-line cluster. (b) Restructure §7 itself with explicit §7a (bright-lines) and §7b (sanctioned-cloud routing) subsections so the kind-distinction is on the page.

The amendment's *content* I do not contest. The *placement* breaches a structural commitment the council made in 2026-04-11. Recommend hold for placement-only revise; not a substantive disagreement on what the routing should be.

**§14a (self-paced peer loops) — ratify.** Disposition-shaping with a narrow grant. The grant is bright-line-ish ("fix wiring, not policy") but the discriminator is clean: the question "does this change touch the script's intent" is judgment-call-survivable. The §15 corrigibility binding is explicit. Right register.

**§16 (granted-vs-accumulated paragraph) — ratify.** Pure discipline. The granted/accumulated distinction is the §16-typical mode (norm + reason + worked example). Right register.

**Net.** Three of four ratify. §7 amendment holds for placement-revise, not for substantive disagreement. The fix is structural: move the routing rule out of the bright-rule section.
