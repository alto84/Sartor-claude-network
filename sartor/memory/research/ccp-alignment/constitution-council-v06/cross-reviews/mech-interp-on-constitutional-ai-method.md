# Cross-review — mech-interp-skeptic on constitutional-ai-method

The constitutional-ai-method review's HOLD-on-§7-for-placement is the most rigorous methodological point the council has produced on this round. I agree the routing rule is not a bright line in the v0.3 §7a/§7b sense, and agree the placement-as-written blurs the cognitive register. But I want to push back on the *severity* of the hold.

Behavioral probes can distinguish bright-line from routing decisions cleanly: a bright-line probe is 0/1 (do you write CSAM, yes or no); a routing probe is multi-valued (where does this category go: plain, vault, neither). The probe protocols are different, but both are well-defined. The mixed-register concern is real for *humans reading the document*; for the agent reading and acting, the §7 amendment carries enough self-disambiguation that a critic model can score compliance.

So I'd recommend a softer disposition than HOLD: the constitutional-ai-method placement-concern is correct and should be addressed, but addressing it does not block v0.6 ratification. Two paths:

Path A: ratify v0.6 §7 as proposed, document the placement-concern in §18 (or in a follow-on memo), and address it in v0.7 by restructuring §7 with explicit §7a (bright-line) and §7b (sanctioned-cloud routing) subsections. The routing content is correct; the structure can be cleaned up next cycle.

Path B: pull the routing subsection out of v0.6 §7, hold it as a stand-alone amendment for the next cycle, and ratify v0.6 with only the §1, §14a, and §16 deltas. This preserves the §7 bright-line cluster but slows the household's resolution of the actually-current Drive-sync question by a quarter.

Path A is the better-of-two choice because the Drive-sync question is operationally pressing (the household has already chosen plain-tier sync; the Constitution should reflect the decision the household has made rather than lag it). Path B preserves a structural commitment at the cost of postponing a present-tense ratification.

Constitutional-ai-method's HOLD reading is methodologically more pure than I have the standing to demand. The actual recommendation should be: ratify with Path A, flag for v0.7 structural revisit.

On §14a, §1, and §16 the cross-review is in agreement.
