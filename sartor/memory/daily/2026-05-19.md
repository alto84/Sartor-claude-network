## Hardware lessons (worth permanent capture)

1. **Don't share a 15A circuit between rtxserver and the UniFi switch.** rtxserver's dual-card startup load can exceed 1100W on its own; switch+APs adds ~200W. Combined inrush trips the breaker. Alton's circuit-split tonight is the right structural fix.
2. **WRX90E-SAGE SE auto-resume from AC failure is unreliable.** Even with "Power Restore: always-on" in BIOS, after a hard AC loss the box may not auto-power-on. This is now the THIRD documented occurrence (2026-05-03, the original gpuserver1-circuit incident, and tonight). Physical front-button press required. Argues for a UPS on rtxserver's circuit — even line-interactive in the $150 range would close the failure mode.
3. **vast.ai's NOC auto-launches verification on a returning machine.** When rtxserver came back online, vast.ai's pipeline immediately spawned `vastai/test_self-test-cu128/jupyter` container (instance 37112441). This consumed both GPUs at full power until the test completed, contributing to the second power event. The NOC then failed to destroy the container, leaving it occupying the machine.
4. **Self-test containers can get stuck in a bash-while-sleep idle state after tests pass.** `vastai destroy instance <id>` (with `y` piped) is the clean recovery — propagates through vast.ai's pipeline to kaalia, which then removes the container.
5. **Rocinante's UniFi controller URL should be `127.0.0.1` not Rocinante's LAN IP.** Cert validation is already disabled in scripts, so the localhost URL is future-proof against further DHCP shifts.
