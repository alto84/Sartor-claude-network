### Part B — Structural additions: three of four

**§12a trust ladder progression triggers** — inserted at end of §12 before §13 break. ~260 words. Domain-scoped promotion. Three criteria:
1. Reliability evidence with a per-domain window duration.
2. Surfaced Stage-5 proposal from the agent.
3. Alton grant in a `feedback_*.md` file with specific scope and expiry.

*Deliberate deviation from delta memo:* the memo's proposed 90-day default window was dropped as arbitrary. The review window is now set per-domain at the time of grant, with explicit guidance that higher-stakes domains get longer windows and "silence does not promote." This is a judgment call made under Alton's 2026-04-19 license to "decide additional issues." The deviation makes the section more defensible against a future challenge that the number was picked without evidence.

**§14a The Operating Agreement and peer-machine governance** — inserted after "Trust evaluation of AI-sourced claims" at end of §14 before §15. ~300 words. Establishes superordinate Constitution > operational Agreement. Names Rocinante and gpuserver1 as current peer set, Blackwell as incoming. Distinguishes peer agents from subagents, cross-references §4 honesty.

**§14b Inter-peer disagreement** — inserted immediately after §14a. ~130 words. Silent override = Constitutional violation. Escalation via Agreement §7 ladder. Urgent override permitted with logged reason. Persistent pattern triggers §18 review.

**§11a "When idle is a failure" — DEFERRED to v0.4.**
The delta memo's own author flagged this as procedurally premature: the rule presupposed an alert-channel that did not exist at proposal time. That alert channel — the hourly heartbeat written atomically from `stale-detect.sh` — went live at `2026-04-20T01:15:50Z`, about 3 hours before this ratification. Having enforcement substrate live for 3 hours does not give enough confidence to encode the enforcement rule into the foundational document. Revisit in v0.4 after the heartbeat has a track record.
