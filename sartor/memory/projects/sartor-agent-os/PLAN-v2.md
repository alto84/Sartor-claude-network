# HEARTH v2.0 — built from scenes

> [!info] **Method.** Built overnight 2026-04-26 from a walk-through of five scenes in the household's actual life. Each plan item traces to a specific sentence in a scene. No item exists for architectural reasons. Per the four CLAUDE.md Discipline principles imported 2026-04-26: every changed line traces to the request; minimum that solves the problem; touch only what serves the request; success criterion pre-stated per item.

> [!warning] Scenes are presented as plausible reconstructions of lived moments based on FAMILY.md / ALTON.md / daily logs. Where I'm inferring rather than knowing, I mark `[inferred]`. Correct any scene that's wrong; the plan items derived from a wrong scene need re-derivation.

## Scene 1 — Tuesday, 6:45 AM, kitchen counter

The 7:35 from Bloomfield is in 50 minutes. Alton has coffee; Aneeta is upstairs; the kids are up but not yet downstairs. He has six minutes before he has to start packing his bag. He opens his phone to whatever HEARTH put there overnight.

What he needs to know in 90 seconds: did anything break overnight; what does today actually need from him; is there a Solar-ITC fire he has to call Jonathan about; is the GPU rented or not; is anyone in the family carrying something he should know about today (test, doctor, performance, deadline).

What he does NOT need: a recap of what HEARTH did. A list of agent invocations. A score. A score about scores. Architecture diagrams. Anything that requires more than one swipe.

What surfaces if it's a normal day: green status line; today's calendar block in plain English; Solar ITC D-N days; one bullet about the family ("Vishala has gym at 10:30; pickup at 3"); one bullet about the business ("GPU at 0% occupancy day 64; recommended bump from gpuserver1 inbox awaiting review").

What surfaces on a yellow day: anomaly in red at the top, what's being done about it, who else needs to know, what action it's asking him to take.

**Plan items derived from Scene 1:**

| # | Item | Success criterion |
|---|---|---|
| S1.1 | The breakfast page exists at one URL on the LAN. Loads in <2s on his phone. Composed of: status line, today's calendar, Solar ITC countdown, one family bullet, one business bullet. | He opens it three mornings in a row and reads it without scrolling. |
| S1.2 | "Today actually needs from you" line is generated by family-scheduler + tax-strategist; not aspirational. If nothing needs him, the line says so explicitly. | The line is correct three days in a row when audited against his calendar. |
| S1.3 | Anomaly mode: when daily-household-health flags yellow+, the breakfast page replaces the normal layout with the anomaly + action + escalation channel. Not a banner; the whole page. | A `kill -9` on the curator triggers the anomaly view next morning. |

## Scene 2 — Saturday, 2:00 AM, Aneeta home from call

Aneeta walks in the front door. She has been awake since 6 AM Friday. The house is dark; the kids and Alton are asleep. She wants to know two things: did anything happen with the kids while she was gone; is anything broken that means she can't sleep through to 9 AM tomorrow.

She opens her phone. What HEARTH should NOT do: narrate her absence back to her ("you missed Vasu's bath; Vayu finished his math"). What HEARTH should NOT show: a dashboard about her work (Neurvati firewall is hard). What it CAN show, if she chose to opt in earlier: a one-line "all kids and pets safe; no escalations" or "Vayu had a hard moment around 8 PM, Alton handled it." Past-tense, brief, no interpretation.

She does not look at the GPU business. She does not look at Solar ITC. Those are Alton's. The breakfast page she sees, if she opts in, is HER breakfast page — different content, different filter.

## Scene 3 — Wednesday, 4:30 PM, Vayu and the math worksheet

Vayu is at the kitchen table. Partial quotients, again. He's frustrated. Roshni Shah's email about him is in the household memory; the worksheets are attached. Alton is on the train, about an hour from home; Aneeta is at Neurvati. Amarkanth is in the living room.

Vayu has not directly engaged with HEARTH yet. He might, eventually, in a year or two. For now: HEARTH does not engage him. It does not appear on a screen near him. It does not nudge him. The parents have not authorized direct kid access; the Constitution §10 future-direct-access section is explicit that this requires a household decision that hasn't happened.

What HEARTH CAN do, with parent authorization: track that home practice happened (Alton or Aneeta logs it; HEARTH records the cadence). Surface trends to the parents weekly: "Vayu did partial-quotient practice 2x this week, 0x last week."

What HEARTH CANNOT do: tutor. Suggest he keep going. Offer hints. Be a sibling that undermines him to his parents. Be a surveillance instrument.

## Scene 4 — Friday, 11:55 PM, gpuserver1 stops responding

The vast.ai listing's heartbeat is stale. The gpuserver1 inbox stops updating. The Calendar/Gmail OAuth token may or may not be alive. Alton is asleep. Aneeta is asleep. The kids are asleep. Loki is curled up somewhere.

Without any out-of-band channel, the household discovers this Saturday morning when Alton checks the breakfast page. That's the 2026-04-22 cable-pull pattern. Forty-eight hours.

With an out-of-band channel: the dead-mans-switch.txt file Alton greps at 7 AM has not been updated since 11:55 PM. The breakfast page opens to the anomaly view. The page itself names the gap ("monitoring stopped 7h35m ago; cause unknown; here's what to check"). If SMS or LED is wired in too, those fire as well.

Critical: HEARTH does NOT page Alton at midnight. The household has not authorized waking him up. Phones-down hours are a household norm. The agent surfaces in the morning through the channel Alton checks anyway.

After a deliberate `kill -9` test, the file goes stale and the breakfast page surfaces the gap by next morning. |

## What v2.0 does NOT contain

Items the walk-through DID NOT generate:

- "Phases A through F" with exit criteria. Scenes don't need phase scaffolding; the calendar does the sequencing.
- A six-persona critique apparatus. v2.0 still uses critics (Cato, Marginalia, Vigil, Aneeta-proxy etc.) but as judges of the artifact, not as load-bearing structure of the plan itself.
- Multi-machine theatrics. Three peer machines are infrastructure, not plan content. They appear in scenes only when a scene actually requires them (Scene 4: gpuserver1).
- A trust-ladder narrative. The trust-ladder is in the Constitution; the plan applies it where stakes warrant it. No need to recapitulate the ladder.
- The "we built up the personality work" branch as a major phase. Personality work is one item (F1), gated on substrate.
- An external-framework-critic round. The four-principle import already happened (CLAUDE.md Discipline section); v2.0 was built under those principles, not retrofitted.
