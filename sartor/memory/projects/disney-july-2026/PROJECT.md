## Outstanding decisions

1. **Which parks which day?** With a 2-day 1-park-per-day ticket, Alton must choose. The group plan suggests:
   - **Fri Jul 17** → California Adventure (at least one family is going)
   - **Sat Jul 18** → Disneyland (group "park day" with Blue Bayou dinner)
   - This means **Thursday (arrival) and Sunday (departure) are non-park days.** That matches: Thu is DTF lunch + character dinner (no park needed for DTF), Sun is fly home. Default recommendation: CA Adventure Fri, Disneyland Sat.
2. **Character dining Thursday evening — yes or no?** Strongly recommend yes for Vasu. He is the primary reason the trip exists (older kids not coming). Character dinner is exactly the experience that makes it memorable for a 4-year-old.
3. **Blue Bayou Saturday — yes.** Alison / Smiths want it, Lindsey excited. Alton should tell Nicol yes so she can book at the 60-day mark.
4. **Flight airport — LAX or SNA?** SNA (John Wayne, Orange County) is physically closer to Anaheim (~20 min vs 40 min from LAX), but has fewer direct routes from EWR/JFK. Check JetBlue, United, Delta. Decision criterion: direct non-stop is worth ~$100 extra; with a 4-year-old, avoid connections.
5. **Rental car vs rideshare?** If just the 3 of them staying at Disneyland Hotel (walkable to parks) with DTF lunch Thursday and character dinner Thursday also at Downtown Disney, rideshare-only is viable. If any off-property meals or a day trip (beach, Bruckers' place), rent a car. Default recommendation: rideshare — simpler with a 4-year-old.
6. **Rest-day / water-park day?** With only Fri + Sat in parks and Sun as departure, there is no rest day needed. Vasu will be exhausted — plan for a hotel pool afternoon on Friday if CA Adventure opens early.
7. **PhotoPass / Memory Maker?** ~$169 add-on for unlimited photos. For a once-in-a-childhood trip with the one young kid, recommend yes.
