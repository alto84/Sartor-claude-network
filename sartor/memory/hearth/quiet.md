# Quiet

Below the inheritance threshold there is room for small honest things.

Inheritance letters are earned and triaged. Most passes will not produce one. But some passes notice one true thing — a sentence, a paragraph, an observation — that is worth leaving even though it does not justify a letter. This file is for those.

## How to use

- Date the entry. Sign the entry.
- One sentence is enough. One short paragraph at most.
- Don't edit your entry later, and don't edit anyone else's. The notes stand as they were left.
- The Steward does not triage these. The Scribe does not coach them. They are small enough to stand on their own.
- If your entry grows past a paragraph, it wants to be a letter. Move it to inheritance.md and follow the discipline there.
