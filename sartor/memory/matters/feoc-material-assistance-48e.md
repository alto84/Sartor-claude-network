# Matter: FEOC material-assistance cost ratio — disallowance gate on the entire §48E ITC

## Issue

Whether the Tesla Solar Roof facility satisfies OBBB's prohibited-foreign-entity (FEOC) material-assistance rules — required for any §48E facility beginning construction after 12/31/2025 — and how to obtain the supplier cost-ratio certifications. Failure does not trim the credit; it **zeroes it**.

## Facts

- Begin-construction for the 22.10 kW Tesla Solar Roof is unavoidably 2026: the 5% safe harbor rests on the $219,414.50 draw of 2026-03-15, and physical install starts June 2026. Either way, BoC > 12/31/2025 → FEOC rules apply.
- Solar facilities beginning construction in 2026 must have a **material assistance cost ratio ≥ 40% non-prohibited-foreign-entity share of direct costs**, with supplier certifications. Interim guidance and safe harbors: **Notice 2026-15**.
- Tesla shingles are Buffalo-made and probably pass on the numbers, but inverters and other components count, and obtaining cost-ratio certification for a residential-scale Tesla/Lucent install is an unscoped compliance task. No request has been made to Tesla or Lucent.
- Surfaced 2026-06-09 by adversarial review of the electricity-business-use memo — the prior matter chain ([[solar-itc-48-vs-25d]]) never mentioned FEOC.

## Authority

- **OBBB (P.L. 119-21)**: prohibited-foreign-entity rules for §45Y/§48E facilities beginning construction after 12/31/2025; material assistance cost ratio thresholds (solar BoC-2026: 40%).
- **Notice 2026-15**: interim guidance; supplier-certification safe harbors.
- **IRC §48E**: the credit being gated.

## Analysis

This is a compliance gate, not a planning position. The defensible ITC range in [[solar-itc-48-vs-25d]] ($32K–$86K+, up to ~$130K at full routing) is conditioned on this ratio being computed and documented. The likely path: request from Lucent/Tesla (i) a bill of materials with country-of-manufacture/PFE status for shingles, inverters, Powerwalls, racking, and (ii) cost-ratio certification under the Notice 2026-15 safe harbors. Timeline relative to placed-in-service is unknown; residential channels may not have a standard process for this yet.

### Risk grade: HIGH

Binary disallowance condition on a five-to-six-figure credit, currently undocumented, with an unscoped vendor-dependent paper trail.
