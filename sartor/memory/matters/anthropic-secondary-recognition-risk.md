# Matter: Anthropic public warning on unauthorized secondary sales — recognition risk on Sartor SPV holdings

## Issue

On 2026-05-12 Anthropic published a support-site article ("Unauthorized Anthropic stock sales and investment scams", https://support.claude.com/en/articles/13704655) declaring that *"any sale or transfer of Anthropic stock... that has not been approved by our Board of Directors is void"* and explicitly naming eight firms as unauthorized — **Hiive among them**. EquityZen is not named, but the article states no authorized secondary marketplaces exist for retail investors and specifically flags SPV/fund-of-fund structures claiming "indirect access" as a red flag pattern.

Alton holds two Anthropic-exposure positions through exactly these patterns:
1. **EquityZen Growth Technology Fund II LLC, Series 13** ($105,037.51 cost basis, Feb 2025). Indirect Anthropic Series E-1 Preferred via Investee Vehicle SPV. EquityZen not named in Anthropic article.
2. **HII Anthropic-01** (a series of HII Anthropic LLC), 300 shares beneficial to Alton ($94,014.90 basis, Dec 2025). **Hiive is named.**

This matter tracks whether Anthropic's recognition stance could impair value at liquidity, and what defensive steps to take.

## Facts

- Both holdings are real (real subscription documents, real wires, real Investee Vehicles or LLCs). This is **not** a fraud situation — the SPVs exist and hold something.
- EquityZen Subscription Agreement §6(o) acknowledges the underlying transfer-restriction issue and provides for refund if ROFR is exercised. The deal funded Feb 12, 2025; the Subscription Amount was not refunded; the Underlying Agreement was completed within the 90-day window per Sub Agreement §4. Per EquityZen's 2025-03-17 investor-portal update, the third-party manager finalized the share acquisition on March 17, 2025 ("investment officially closed"). This indicates EquityZen's Investee Vehicle acquired *something* — but the Sub Agreement never asserts Anthropic's affirmative approval, only that ROFR wasn't exercised to block.
- **EquityZen Investment Solution Type is "Standard Third-Party Interest"** (per 2025-03-17 portal update). Series 13 does NOT hold Anthropic stock directly, even at one remove — it holds an interest in a third-party manager's Investee Vehicle, which in turn holds the shares. Multi-layer fund-of-fund. The identity of the third-party Investee Vehicle manager is **not disclosed** to Series Members in any document Alton has on file. **This is the structural pattern Anthropic's 2026-05-12 article specifically disclaims.** If the third-party manager turns out to be one of the named-and-flagged firms (Open Door Partners, Unicorns Exchange, Pachamama, Lionheart Ventures, Hiive, Forge, Sydecar, Upmarket), then EquityZen Series 13 has indirect exposure even though EquityZen itself is not named. **Forge and Sydecar are well-known third-party SPV-manager brands** — either could plausibly be the underlying manager. This is a top-of-list question for the securities-attorney consult.
- **Anthropic Series F round (Aug 2025)** moved post-money valuation from $61.5B to $183B (~3.0x in 7 months) per EquityZen's 2025-09-26 portal update. Series E-1 (Alton's exposure) is now junior to Series F by liquidation preference. The Series F round's size and valuation may have been the precipitating event for Anthropic's 2026-05-12 anti-secondary public stance — at $100B+ valuations, companies typically formalize their anti-secondary posture.
- EquityZen Risk Factors (Exhibit B) explicitly disclose: *"It is not anticipated that any Investee Vehicle Securities acquired by the Series will be marketable"* and *"The Interests are highly illiquid, have no public market, and generally are not transferable, except with the consent of the Manager... and compliance with applicable securities laws."*
- Hiive HII Anthropic-01 closed Dec 24, 2025 at $298.46/share — different class structure than EquityZen Series E-1 Preferred at $61.98 implied. The MOU memorializing the 300/300/300 family split was drafted same day but is **only signed by Alton (Emmett)** — Alton Oliver Sartor (father) and Belinda Marascalco Sartor (mother) signature lines remain blank in the file on record per [[reference_anthropic_shares]].
- EquityZen Subscription Amount was wired from a Fidelity Joint WROS account Z25598998 (presumably joint with Aneeta), confirmation 79277892, 2025-02-10. Legal title to the Series Interest is in Emmett's name only — the joint-account funding source is a marital-property nuance, cross-referenced in [[anthropic-721b-investment-company]].
- AZ Compliance disclosure of both holdings is an outstanding action item per [[reference_anthropic_shares]] (not yet confirmed complete as of 2026-05-12).

## Authority

- **DGCL §202** (Restrictions on transfer and ownership of securities) — Delaware corporate law governing the enforceability of transfer restrictions imposed by a corporation's charter and stockholder agreements. A transfer in violation of a properly noted restriction may be void as to the company.
- **Securities Act of 1933 §4(a)(2)** and **Regulation D Rule 506** — Private-placement exemption under which the Series Interests are offered. Anthropic's Series E-1 Preferred itself was issued under §4(a)(2); secondary transfers without registration require an exemption.
- **Anthropic, PBC support article 2026-05-12** — Public corporate position: transfers without Board approval are void; eight firms named as unauthorized (Open Door Partners, Unicorns Exchange, Pachamama, Lionheart Ventures, **Hiive**, Forge, Sydecar, Upmarket).
- **EquityZen Subscription Agreement §6(o)** — Series Interest itself is non-transferable except in accordance with Operating Agreement, with Manager consent (which may be withheld), and with counsel opinion that an exemption is available.

## Analysis

### What "void" actually means

Under DGCL §202, a transfer of stock in violation of a properly-noted transfer restriction is "void" *as to the corporation*. The corporation does not have to recognize the purported transferee as a shareholder. This is enforceable against the SPV at the corporation level — Anthropic can refuse to update its stock ledger, refuse to pay distributions to the SPV, and refuse to recognize the SPV's rights at IPO/liquidity.

What it does NOT do automatically:

- It does **not** void the underlying contract between the SPV and the original seller. That contract may still be enforceable as a matter of New York contract law (the governing law for EquityZen Subscription Agreements; Hiive structure presumably similar).
- It does **not** mean the SPV has no economic claim — many SPV structures use forward contracts where the original seller retains title and contracts to deliver shares or proceeds at liquidity. Anthropic's recognition stance doesn't moot a contractual obligation between buyer and seller, unless the seller's underlying agreement with Anthropic (option agreement, RSU agreement, or shareholder agreement) made the seller's own commitment to forward proceeds also void.
- It does **not** mean money is gone today.

### Risk tier by holding

**EquityZen Series 13 ($105K Alton basis):**
- EquityZen is not named in the Anthropic article. Founded 2013, long operating history; some industry signal suggests they negotiate case-by-case with issuers or use cleaner structures.
- Cost basis $61.98/share-equivalent is a 10.5% premium over Anthropic Series E-1 issue price ($56.09 in Jan 2025). Real-economy seller premium consistent with legitimate secondary purchase.
- Likely outcomes at Anthropic liquidity (qualitative estimates, **not validated probabilities**):
  - *Best case (~50%):* Anthropic recognizes the Investee Vehicle (original sellers had transfer rights or got Anthropic clearance). SPV gets paid; Series Member gets net of fees.
  - *Middle case (~35%):* Anthropic doesn't recognize SPV, but SPV's contracts against original sellers are enforceable. Cash flows to SPV with delay and/or modest legal-cost compression. Series Member gets 70–85% of expected payout.
  - *Worst case (~15%):* SPV recognition fails AND original-seller contracts unenforceable. Total or near-total loss. Triggers §165(g) worthless-securities claim for capital loss.

**Hiive HII Anthropic-01 ($94K Alton-beneficial / $282K total family):**
- Hiive is named in the Anthropic article. This is a public corporate position taken specifically against Hiive structures.
- Risk profile is meaningfully worse than EquityZen on the recognition dimension (qualitative estimates, **not validated probabilities**):
  - *Best case (~30%):* Hiive structure works (forward contracts, cash-only economics, claim against original sellers' distributions). Family gets 60–90% of expected payout.
  - *Middle case (~40%):* Litigation between Hiive and Anthropic or between Hiive and original sellers. Lengthy resolution. Final payout uncertain.
  - *Worst case (~30%):* SPV interest is uncollectable. Total loss of $282K family stake.

These ranges are working qualitative estimates from public legal-structure reasoning, not a securities-attorney opinion. The actual risk is what an attorney would price after reviewing Hiive's and EquityZen's underlying purchase agreements with original sellers — documents Alton does not currently have visibility into.

### Risk grade: MEDIUM

Why not HIGH:
- No present harm. Liquidity event is the trigger; Anthropic IPO is not imminent.
- EquityZen exposure is not directly named.
- Substrate of the SPVs is real (not a fraud-zero case).
- Recoverable losses are bounded by basis (~$199K Alton-attributable), not unbounded.

Why not LOW:
- $199K Alton-attributable basis is real-money exposure.
- Hiive being directly named is a credible adverse corporate-record event.
- AZ Compliance dimension is non-trivial — being a named-and-flagged structure could become a future disclosure issue.
- The §721(b) cross-risk in [[anthropic-721b-investment-company]] makes any move into Solar Inference LLC doubly bad.

## Position

**Hold, don't panic-sell, don't contribute to SI, do consult a securities attorney on the Hiive position.** Three actions:

1. **Securities attorney consult on Hiive structure.** Before any liquidity event, understand:
   - What does HII Anthropic-01 actually hold? Direct shares, forward contracts, or economic interest only?
   - Did Hiive obtain Anthropic ROFR clearance for the original sellers' transfers?
   - If Anthropic refuses to recognize the SPV at liquidity, what claim do Series Members have against Hiive or the original sellers?
   - Estimated cost: $2–5K for an initial opinion from a securities or VC-secondary specialist.

2. **AZ Compliance disclosure.** Confirm both holdings have been disclosed to AstraZeneca Compliance / Conflicts of Interest. If not done, complete within May 2026. The disclosure should explicitly name Hiive (now publicly flagged) and EquityZen, and reference the 2026-05-12 Anthropic public statement.

3. **Hiive MOU execution.** Per [[reference_anthropic_shares]] outstanding action item: get father and mother countersigned copies of the Hiive MOU on file. This is independent of the recognition-risk question but compounds with it — if the family ever needs to enforce the 300/300/300 split (against each other, against Schwab, or against an estate), an only-Emmett-signed MOU is brittle.

**Do not:**

- Panic-sell into a secondary marketplace at a distressed bid.
- Contribute either holding to Solar Inference LLC before securities-attorney opinion + CPA §721(b) opinion + ITC filing acceptance (see [[anthropic-721b-investment-company]]).
- Treat this as a fraud event — it is not. The exposure is recognition-and-enforceability, not fraud-vs-substance.
