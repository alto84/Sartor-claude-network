# Matter: NJ non-conformity to §168(k) bonus depreciation

## Issue

NJ does NOT conform to federal §168(k) bonus depreciation. The federal bonus depreciation on the Tesla Solar Roof and GPU hardware creates a federal/NJ timing difference that needs proper return treatment.

## Facts

- Federal: 100% bonus depreciation under §168(k)(6) for 2026-placed-in-service property (rate to verify post-OBBB).
- Solar Inference LLC: Tesla Solar Roof $438,829 + GPU hardware $37,831 = potential federal bonus depreciation deduction $400K+ in TY2026.
- NJ: Decouples from §168(k). Property depreciates under MACRS with regular schedules.
- Sartor TY2025 LLC return: minor (~$5K) since pre-revenue and assets not yet in service.
- TY2026 will be the year the difference shows up.

## Authority

- **N.J.S.A. 54A:5-1.2**: NJ Gross Income Tax decoupling from federal bonus depreciation.
- **NJ Form GIT-DEP**: Computation of NJ depreciation when federal differs.

## Analysis

For Solar Inference LLC TY2026:
- Federal: ~$373K bonus depreciation deduction in year 1
- NJ: ~$15-20K MACRS depreciation deduction in year 1 (5- or 7-year property)

Federal/NJ K-1 will show the same gross income but different depreciation expense. Each member's NJ return needs Form GIT-DEP adjustment.

The federal benefit is real and durable. NJ tax higher in years 1-5 then reverses (NJ catches up over MACRS schedule). Net economic outcome: federal benefit dominates.
