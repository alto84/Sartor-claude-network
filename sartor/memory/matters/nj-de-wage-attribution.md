# Matter: NJ vs DE wage-source attribution post-NYC role transition

## Issue

W-2 still shows DE address with DE-only state withholding as of 2026-04-13 per JF, despite Alton's role transition to NYC office (Empire State Building) starting ~2026-03-31. Need to update HR record AND determine appropriate state of withholding for the NYC role.

## Facts

- AZ Senior Medical Director, AI Innovation and Validation, NYC Empire State Building. Started ~2026-03-31. Remote 2 days/week, on-site NYC 3 days/week.
- Prior role: AZ Delaware office.
- TY2025: all wages earned in DE per Alton's email to JF 2026-04-13.
- TY2026 forward: 3 days NYC, 2 days remote (NJ).
- W-2 still shows DE address (per JF 2026-04-13).
- NJ resident. No NJ/DE reciprocal agreement.

## Authority

- **N.J.S.A. 54A:5-1**: NJ residents taxed on all income worldwide. NJ-source income for nonresidents only.
- **30 Del.C. §1124**: DE income tax on nonresidents — wages for services performed in DE.
- **NY Tax Law §601**: NY income tax on nonresidents — wages for services performed in NY.
- **NJ income tax credit**: §54A:4-1 — credit for taxes paid to other states.

## Analysis

Three states potentially in play:

1. **NJ (resident)**: All wages taxable. Always.
2. **DE (nonresident)**: Wages for actual DE work. After 2026-03-31, no DE work — DE withholding inappropriate.
3. **NY (nonresident)**: Wages for NYC work. 3 days/week × ~50 weeks = 150+ days NYC work in TY2026. NY withholding required.

If W-2 continues DE withholding into 2026:
- NJ refund needed (DE tax credit on NJ return).
- NY underpayment — NY will demand its share. Likely penalties.

Alton needs HR to:
1. Update home address to NJ (already known)
2. Update work location to NY (NYC role)
3. Switch state withholding from DE to **NY** (work state) — NJ then credits via §54A:4-1.

### Risk grade: LOW

Process issue, not position issue. Underpayment penalty risk to NY if not corrected. Refund/credit cleanup at TY2026 filing if W-2 reports correctly.
