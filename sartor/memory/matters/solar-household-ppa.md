# Matter: Intra-family power-purchase arrangement — the ~100% business-use increment

## Issue

Whether Solar Inference LLC can sell the solar roof's non-GPU generation to the Sartor household under a written PPA at the PSE&G retail tariff rate — sweeping net-metering credit value into LLC revenue and pushing the §48E business-use fraction from the metered Tier 1 level (~70–90% with rig3 + Powerwalls) toward ~100%.

## Facts

- Structure (from the 2026-06-09 electricity-business-use memo): LLC owns the facility; written PPA obligates the household to buy all generation not consumed by the GPU fleet at the actual PSE&G tariff rate (~$0.26/kWh area — document from a real bill; fleet.yaml's $0.18 is a placeholder); metered, invoiced monthly, paid in cash from the personal joint account to the LLC's Chase account.
- Why it's needed at all: NJ net-metering credits offset the retail bill of the utility customer of record — the spouses' personal residential account. Without the PPA, export value accrues to the household and the export fraction collapses back to personal use. PSE&G account restructuring to the LLC is likely impractical for a residence.
- Scale check (verdict-corrected): 20–35% of 19,854 kWh/yr at $0.26 is ~$1,032–$1,807/yr of PPA revenue. Annual true-up residual pays only ~$0.03–0.05/kWh.
- The increment at stake: roughly $100K of ITC plus ~$283K of additional year-1 depreciation between the 24% GPU-only fraction and ~100% (per the memo's quantification table; all subject to the §48E(b)(2)(A)(ii) roof-denominator question).

## Authority

- FMV pricing at the published utility tariff is the best possible comparable; no per-se related-party disallowance.
- **Counterweights for respecting the form:** genuine third-party business (vast.ai revenue, SRECs, true-up payments — Moline Properties), objective market price, cash actually moving (Frank Lyon).
- **Against:** Gregory v. Helvering substance-over-form (collapsible to "homeowners consuming their own roof's power"); §7701(o) economic-substance risk concentrates on the PPA increment, whose principal effect is tax; IRS litigated hard against 1980s self-leased energy-property shelters. **No case or ruling directly blesses a residential solar self-PPA.**
- **§280A**: grey — better reading is it does not reach a partnership's own affixed equipment, but the Solar Roof IS the dwelling's roof, inviting a §280A(c)(6)-flavored rental-to-self argument. No controlling authority.
- **§50(a)**: 5-year recapture if the structure is later unwound to personal use. Note (verdict-corrected): §50(b)(2)(D) is NOT available as affirmative support — it covers §48-defined "energy property" and was never conformed to §48E.

## Analysis

This is the contested Tier 2 increment in [[solar-itc-48-vs-25d]]'s two-tier strategy. Tier 1 (metered GPU consumption + LLC-contracted export/SREC income) stands on its own; the PPA buys the last ~20–35% of fraction for ~$1,000–1,800/yr of circular cash flow whose principal effect is tax. It is an aggressive position without direct authority.

Prerequisites if taken: LLC owns the system at PIS; SREC registration in LLC name; revenue-grade production meter + household submeter (or computed residual); executed PPA before PIS; monthly invoices + bank-cleared payments; profit-motive pricing maintained (§183 hygiene — never frame kWh-burning as ITC-justified); insurance posture reconciled ([[insurance-commercial-coverage]] — cannot tell the IRS "commercial property" and Selective "residence").

### Risk grade: HIGH

Correct-but-undocumented is not the problem; the problem is no direct authority respects a married couple's LLC selling roof power to the same couple's kitchen.

## Position

**Do not execute without JF's written sign-off.** Build the Tier 1 stack regardless (it is required for the PPA anyway and carries most of the value). Treat the PPA as a go/no-go JF decision with the recapture exposure priced.
