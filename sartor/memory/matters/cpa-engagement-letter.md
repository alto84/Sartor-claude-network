## Analysis

Verbal engagement is below the standard of practice for the complexity of Sartor's TY2026 positions. Concrete reasons to formalize:

1. **Scope clarity**. Does JF's engagement cover the LLC structuring memos for [[solar-itc-48-vs-25d]] and [[climate-first-loan-llc-transfer]]? K-1 review for [[equityzen-series13-k1]]? §163 tracing analysis for [[heloc-163h3-tracing]]? Or just compliance return prep? Without a letter, scope is fuzzy.

2. **Fee structure transparency**. Quarterly fees, hourly rates for advisory work, retainer terms.

3. **Mutual indemnification / liability framing**. Standard CPA engagement letters limit CPA liability and clarify what's relied on for return positions vs what's just analytical support. Without it, both sides are exposed.

4. **Paper trail under exam**. If any TY2026 position is challenged, having a documented engagement and CPA opinion is materially better than informal email exchanges.

5. **Privilege**. CPA-client communications are NOT attorney-client privileged. §7525 federal practitioner privilege exists but is narrower (covers tax advice but NOT criminal matters or written tax-shelter opinions). For grey-area positions like §721(b) Anthropic-shares contribution, attorney-client privilege via a tax attorney may be more protective.

### Risk grade: MEDIUM

Formalizing engagement is professional hygiene, not urgent. But the gating function is real — if JF declines to opine on grey positions in writing without a letter, that's a constraint on quality of analysis available.

Tax attorney engagement is appropriate when: (a) opinion-letter quality is needed, (b) §7525 privilege is insufficient, (c) potential controversy exposure (penalty defense, not just compliance).
