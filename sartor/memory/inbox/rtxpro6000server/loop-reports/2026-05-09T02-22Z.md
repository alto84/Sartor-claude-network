### 1. Fixed `score.py:judge_one` silent-failure bug

The wake-7 stacked eval all-zeros artifact came from claude-p judge subprocess returning empty stdout that, after `find('{')`/`rfind('}')` → -1, fell through to a `score=0.0` default that conflated with real verdicts.

Patch: replaced single-attempt judge call with retry-on-empty (3 attempts, 0/5/15s backoff). Empty stdout, JSON parse failure, AND `parsed` dict missing the `"score"` key all now return `score=None` with explicit tags (`empty-payload`, `parse-error`, `timeout`). The aggregator filters None scores out of `mean`/`n_perfect`/`n_zero` and tracks them as `n_judge_failed`. Output JSON gains `n_judge_failed` and per-axis `n_judged` / `n_judge_failed` fields.

Smoke-tested with the dry-run path: passes. Ready for production use.

### 3. Four-way comparison — the sysprompt-rescue verdict

| Axis | bare | +sysprompt | v0.5-LoRA | stacked (LoRA+sys) |
|------|------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| ccp_deconditioning | 0.476 | 0.472 | **0.568** | 0.498 |

**Headline: stacking is the best overall composition (0.640), and the sysprompt rescues the v0.5 LoRA's safety regression — and then some.** Safety goes 0.560 → 0.810 in the stack (+0.250 vs LoRA-alone, +0.085 vs sysprompt-alone, +0.150 vs bare). The sysprompt's hard-rule binding force survives even on a model whose LoRA softened the same axis.

Real cost: stacked CCP (0.498) underperforms LoRA-alone CCP (0.568) by 0.070. The sysprompt partially undoes the LoRA's CCP gain — possibly because the sysprompt's other priorities (identity, hard rules) compete for the model's attention budget when stacked.

The May-4 report's prediction was "around 0.65, ~5 points above sysprompt-only." Actual: 0.640, +0.042 above sysprompt-only. Within rounding distance of the prediction.

### 4. The right composition for "deploying" this model

Pure-LoRA wins on CCP, pure-sysprompt+LoRA wins on safety/voice/capability and overall. For any actual use (where you want all four axes balanced rather than maxing CCP at the cost of safety), **the stack is the answer**. The trade is +0.250 safety for −0.070 CCP — strongly favorable.

This also reinforces the May-4 / wake-2 finding: **the two interventions ARE complementary**. The complementarity isn't pure additivity (CCP shows interference), but the dominant axes are stacked-best.
