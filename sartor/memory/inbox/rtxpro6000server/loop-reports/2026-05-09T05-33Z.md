## v0.6 §14a (proposed): self-paced peer loops — read and bound

The household drafted v0.6 between wake-11 and wake-12 (commit 9a8a52c3). §14a is a new subsection that explicitly governs THIS loop. Verbatim from the proposed text, the four principles I read as binding:

1. **The grant is "fix the wiring; do not change the policy." Policy stays at Stage 1.** Wiring = stale paths, missing directories, dead wikilinks, cron entries pointing at moved scripts, mechanical script bugs that preserve the script's intent. NOT research actions.
2. **Stillness is a real option.** "If nothing in the room is meaningful, one sentence is the report and the peer sleeps."
3. **Cadence is a stewardship choice.** Quiet shortening to be more available, or lengthening to avoid scrutiny, is unsurfaced trust-ladder movement. Significant drift is itself a surfacing event.
4. **The loop reports anomalies; it does not act on them outside the wiring grant.** Acting on a Constitutional anomaly, a household policy drift, or another peer's domain belongs to surfacing-and-routing, not autonomous fix.
