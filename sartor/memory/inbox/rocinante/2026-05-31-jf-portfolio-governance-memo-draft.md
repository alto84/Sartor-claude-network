**Question 1 — §1092 straddle deferral / QCC classification.**

My understanding is that a short-term loss on a covered-call buyback is deferred under §1092 to the extent of unrealized gain on the offsetting long stock, unless the call is a "qualified covered call" (QCC) under §1092(c)(4). Most of my short calls are deep ITM and I suspect fail the QCC strike tests, which would defer the losses.

Please classify each of the following by §1092 QCC status and tell me which buyback losses are **allowed in TY2026** vs **deferred**:

- NVDA Jun 18 2026 $140C (×8), Jul 17 2026 $180C (×16), against 2,651 NVDA sh, basis ~$100, spot ~$215
- GOOGL Jul 17 2026 $290C (×5), against 2,466 GOOGL sh, basis ~$186, spot ~$383
- CRWV Jan 21 2028 $70C (×22), against 2,935 CRWV sh, basis ~$110, spot ~$105 — **note CRWV stock is at an unrealized loss; I believe this one is NOT deferred because there's no offsetting gain. Please confirm.**
- NBIS Jan 21 2028 $85C (×6), against 705 NBIS sh, basis ~$95, spot ~$215

**Specific ask:** Of the ~$220K nominal STCL, what dollar amount is deductible in TY2026?
