## Decision considerations — personal vs Solar Inference LLC

Solar Inference LLC is a **50/50 multi-member NJ LLC** (Alton + Aneeta), formed 2025-09-06, EIN 39-4199284. It has been characterized to the CPA and to the IRS as an active operating business (solar-powered AI inference) for purposes of the 30% federal ITC and bonus depreciation on the Tesla Solar Roof. See [[business/solar-inference]] for entity detail.

### Why personal is the safer default

1. **Aneeta would gain indirect 50% beneficial ownership.** Contributing personally-held appreciated assets to a 50/50 LLC without offsetting capital from the other member is, for tax purposes, treated as a contribution to the entity, but for **state-law marital-property and estate purposes**, it can be construed as a transmutation or partial gift of separate property to a jointly-owned vehicle. This is meaningful for both holdings, and load-bearing for the Hiive holding because parents' beneficial interests would also be exposed to the marital LLC structure.

2. **Section 721(b) "investment company" trap.** Section 721 generally lets a member contribute appreciated property to a partnership tax-free, but §721(b) carves out "investment company" partnerships, in which case the contributor must recognize gain on contribution. SI's primary character today is operating (solar + GPU rental), but if SI sits on $400K of Anthropic-private equity and very little operating activity, the IRS could re-characterize it. With Anthropic's mark almost certainly above cost since Feb 2025, this risks immediate gain recognition. **CPA must opine before any contribution.**

3. **The "active business" framing for Solar ITC and bonus depreciation depends on SI looking like an operating entity.** Loading SI with passive Anthropic securities undercuts that posture, particularly while the ITC/depreciation positions are pre-filing and the IRS could examine entity character. The ITC deadline is 2026-07-04. Don't muddy the waters before then.

4. **Hiive holding is governed by a three-party family MOU.** Moving the 300 Emmett-shares to SI without unwinding the nominee structure with Alton Oliver and Belinda first would breach the MOU. Even with consent, putting parents' beneficial interests into a vehicle co-owned by Aneeta creates a complication that should not exist for a clean inheritance plan.

5. **Liquidity is zero** in both holdings. Neither can be sold to fund SI operations without (a) ROFR clearance from Anthropic, (b) Manager consent, and (c) a buyer. SI's ITC/depreciation case does not need these as collateral. There is no SI-side cash benefit.

6. **QSBS (§1202)** is almost certainly **not** available on either holding because both were acquired on the secondary market via SPV/fund structures, not from original issuance by Anthropic. This is a non-factor either way (no benefit lost by keeping personal). Confirm with CPA.

### Where SI ownership might make sense (later, not now)

- After solar is placed in service and the 2026 ITC + bonus-depreciation positions are filed and accepted, the operating-business character question is less time-sensitive.
- If SI accumulates significant operating losses going forward (continued GPU buildout, R&D), passive capital-gain income could be more useful inside SI than personally — though character matching (capital vs ordinary) limits the benefit.
- Estate-planning narrative: a multi-generational Sartor-Saxena holding company is a coherent long-term structure if and only if the Operating Agreement is amended to handle special allocations cleanly and if parents (for Hiive) are willing to assign their nominee interests into the same vehicle.

Effective rates for NJ-resident MFJ at Alton + Aneeta's income level. Canonical rate table lives in [[TAXES]]; reproduced here for quick reference.

| Component | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Federal NIIT | 3.8% | Always — well above $250K MFJ threshold |
| NJ state | 8.97% – 10.75% | NJ taxes cap gains as ordinary income; 10.75% kicks in above $1M MFJ |
| **Combined effective** | **~27.8% – 34.5%** | **Set aside ~35% on any wire** |

Distribution is **pre-tax cash**: EquityZen and Hiive do not withhold. K-1 the following spring (Schedule D, long-term). Quarterly estimated payments (1040-ES) may be needed in the realization year to avoid underpayment penalties.

Worked illustrative example: $100K basis → $500K wire → $400K gain → ~$138K combined tax → ~$262K net retained on the $500K gross.

If shares are distributed in-kind instead of cash (less common), §731 generally treats the distribution as non-taxable receipt with carryover basis — you control timing of the sale and the corresponding tax year.
