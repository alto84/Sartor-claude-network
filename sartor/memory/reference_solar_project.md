## Decision considerations

### Why roll items into the Climate First Loan

- **Tax wrapper:** energy-property items (Powerwalls, possibly wood deck, snow guards, vent reloc) capture 30% ITC if in the LLC contract before placed-in-service.
- **Single-vendor execution:** Lucent owns the integration; one contract, one set of payments.
- **No-payment-period:** the 6-month grace runs to ~2026-07-20; additional draws under the same facility ride that grace too.
- **Climate First's clean-energy mission alignment:** lender is structurally favorable for incremental energy-property additions.

### Why route home-improvement items elsewhere (HELOC or paid)

- **Cleaner separation of basis:** energy-property in the LLC, general home improvement personal. No commingling worries for the IRS.
- **HELOC interest** is generally deductible up to $750K aggregate primary-residence acquisition+improvement debt under TCJA — for a $95K–$170K addition this is workable, and HELOC rates may beat Climate First's ~8.3% APR.
- **Re-underwrite friction:** any increase in the Climate First financed amount triggers Bank approval and likely a re-underwrite. Adding $100K+ to the existing loan is non-trivial.
- **HELOC cash extraction from brokerage** is also an option for items with low ITC value and where Alton wants to avoid encumbering more home equity.

### HELOC as alternative financing source

The active HELOC on 85 Stonebridge — **Georgia's Own Credit Union** lender, **Cenlar** servicer, **Symmetry Lending** funder, **CrossCountry Mortgage** originator, account ending 7887 — is the natural funding lane for items NOT eligible for the Climate First Loan amendment. **Caveat:** the HELOC was largely drawn at closing ($506K wired against $527K limit on 2025-10-23). Available headroom must be confirmed via the Cenlar portal before relying on this lane. If it's near-fully-drawn, the realistic alternatives for non-energy items are brokerage cash extraction or a Climate First re-underwrite.

### The Powerwall question (cleanest item)

Two Powerwalls are unambiguously energy property and unambiguously belong on the Lucent contract — and unambiguously belong in the LLC if the LLC is going to hold the asset for ITC. This is the highest-leverage item: ~$25K of basis × 30% = ~$7,500 of ITC capture if structured right.

### The tree-removal question (already paid, can't redo)

Trees were removed and paid for personally in March 2026 — pre-LLC-transfer. That money is sunk personally. Whether any portion of the ~$4K–$10K paid is includable in solar ITC/depreciation basis is a CPA question; do not assume yes.
