# Solar Inference LLC -- Tax Benefits Reference

Deep reference for ITC, bonus depreciation, and NJ SuSI benefits. See also [work/taxes/reference/depreciation.md](../../taxes/reference/depreciation.md) for the personal/CPA-facing version.

## Federal ITC (IRC §48)

- **Credit rate:** 30% of eligible project cost
- **Basis for calculation:** $438,829 (total Lucent Energy contract)
- **Estimated credit:** ~$131,649
- **Entity claiming:** Solar Inference LLC, flows to members via K-1
- **Basis reduction:** When a 30% ITC is claimed, the depreciable basis is reduced by 50% of the credit (~$65,825)
- **Adjusted depreciable basis:** ~$373,004

The ITC is a passive activity credit for partnership members. Passive activity rules may limit the credit's use against active income. Discuss with Jonathan Francis.

## Bonus Depreciation (IRC §168(k), OBBB 2025-2028)

- **Rate:** 100% in year placed in service (OBBB reinstated 100% for 2025-2028)
- **Adjusted basis after ITC reduction:** ~$373,005
- **Treatment:** Creates a large partnership loss in the year placed in service
- **Pass-through to members:** Each member (Alton, Aneeta) receives 50% of the loss via K-1
- **At-risk rules:** Members must be "at-risk" for the amount of the deduction (First Climate loan terms matter here)
- **Passive activity:** If the LLC is a passive activity for either member, losses may be suspended

## NJ SuSI (Successor Solar Incentive)

- **Program:** New Jersey Board of Public Utilities solar incentive
- **Estimated amount:** ~$24,447 (15-year cumulative; structure can be upfront payment or annual)
- **Application status:** Unknown -- confirm with Jonathan or BPU
- **Tax treatment:** Incentive payments received are taxable LLC income

## Strategy Notes

1. The ITC creates a credit carryforward if it exceeds current year liability. Credits can carry forward 20 years.
2. The bonus depreciation creates a paper loss that flows through to Alton's and Aneeta's personal returns, potentially sheltering W-2 income (subject to passive activity rules).
3. At-risk limitations under IRC §465 may limit the loss if First Climate financing is considered non-recourse.
4. The "placed in service" timing (2025 vs. 2026) is the most critical decision point -- determine with Jonathan.
