# 2. Head of Patient Safety / Pharmacovigilance
> Parent: 1-cmo | Children: 3-qppv, 4-signal-mgmt, 5-pv-ops, 6-risk-mgmt, 7-aggregate-reporting, 8-pv-quality | Cross-ref: 1-cmo (escalation)

## Role
The Head of Patient Safety / Pharmacovigilance holds end-to-end accountability for the safety profile of all marketed and investigational products. This role serves as the organizational authority for benefit-risk evaluation, signal management, regulatory safety reporting, and PV system compliance. For a single-product company (Prosinertimib, EGFR inhibitor, NSCLC), this means maintaining lean but inspection-ready operations across ICSR processing, signal detection, aggregate reporting, risk management, and CRO oversight. The role reports to the CMO and serves as the primary interface with regulatory authorities on safety matters.

## Regulatory Grounding
- EU GVP Modules I (PV systems quality), V (Risk management), VI (ICSR management), IX (Signal management)
- ICH E2A (Clinical safety data management), E2B(R3) (ICSR data elements), E2C(R2) (PSUR/PBRER), E2E (Pharmacovigilance planning), E2F (DSUR)
- FDA 21 CFR 312.32 (IND safety reporting), 21 CFR 314.80 (Post-marketing reporting)
- CIOMS I-VI, VIII (Signal detection)

## Sub-Processes

### a. Daily Safety Oversight
Review incoming ICSR volume, triage queue, and processing compliance metrics each business day. Ensure all serious cases are assessed within regulatory timelines (24h initial assessment for serious ICSRs, 15-day/7-day expedited reporting per 21 CFR 312.32 or EU GVP Module VI). Escalate any fatal or life-threatening unexpected events to CMO immediately.

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] ICSR triage and medical review workflow.

### b. Signal Escalation & Evaluation
Receive signal detection outputs (PRR, ROR, EBGM) from the signal management function and apply clinical judgment to validate or refute signals. For validated signals, convene ad-hoc safety review and determine whether labeling changes, Dear Healthcare Professional Communications (DHPCs), or study modifications are warranted. Maintain the signal tracking log per GVP Module IX with documented rationale for all disposition decisions.

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] Signal validation clinical assessment protocol.

### c. Aggregate Report Cycle Management
Approve the reporting calendar maintained by Associate Director, Aggregate Reporting (7-aggregate-reporting) for PSUR/PBRER (per ICH E2C(R2)), DSUR (per ICH E2F), and PADER. Ensure data lock points are defined 90 days before submission deadlines, review writing assignments, and review final drafts for clinical accuracy and regulatory completeness. For a single-product company, the DSUR is the primary aggregate deliverable during clinical development; PBRER becomes primary post-approval. Operational calendar management and authoring coordination are owned by 7-aggregate-reporting.

### d. Regulatory Authority Interaction
Serve as primary safety contact for FDA, EMA, and national competent authorities. Respond to safety-related questions, RFIs, and Type A meeting requests. Coordinate with Regulatory Affairs on safety-related label negotiations and post-approval commitments. Prepare and present at PRAC rapporteur meetings when required.

### e. CRO / Vendor Oversight
Maintain oversight of all outsourced PV activities per GVP Module I, Section I.C.1 (MAH retains full legal responsibility for all delegated PV activities; outsourced activities must be governed by written agreements, subject to QPPV oversight, and auditable). Conduct quarterly business reviews and periodic audits for all vendors. Conduct cause-for-concern audits when KPI thresholds are breached.

**Vendor KPI Thresholds (escalate to Head of PS if breached for 2 consecutive quarters):**

| Vendor | KPI | Target | Escalation Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| SafetyFirst Ltd (case intake) | Serious case processing time | < 24 hours | > 36 hours |
| SafetyFirst Ltd | Non-serious case processing time | < 5 business days | > 7 business days |
| SafetyFirst Ltd | Data accuracy rate | > 98% | < 95% |
| SafetyFirst Ltd | Query response time | < 48 hours | > 72 hours |
| MedSearch Global (literature) | Screening recall | > 95% | < 90% |
| MedSearch Global | False positive rate | < 30% | > 40% |
| MedSearch Global | Search string coverage | 100% of defined terms | Any gap |
| PharmaLex (LPPV) | Local submission timeliness | 100% on-time | Any late submission |
| Argus Cloud (database) | System uptime | > 99.5% | < 99.0% |
| Argus Cloud | E2B(R3) transmission success rate | > 99% | < 97% |

**Audit schedule:** Annual on-site audits for SafetyFirst Ltd and MedSearch Global. PharmaLex: desk-based audit annually, on-site every 2 years. Argus Cloud: vendor qualification review annually, aligned with system validation lifecycle.

### f. Safety Committee Preparation & Chairing
Chair the internal Safety Management Team (SMT) meeting (minimum monthly; weekly during active clinical enrollment). Ensure meeting minutes capture decisions with assigned actions, owners, and deadlines. Provide safety input to the cross-functional Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) for clinical trials.

**SMT Standing Agenda Template:**
1. **Minutes & actions review** -- prior meeting action item status
2. **Signal management update** -- new signals, validation outcomes, SMT decisions required (see 4-signal-mgmt, Sub-process f)
3. **ICSR compliance metrics** -- 15-day/90-day submission rates, rejection rates, case backlog
4. **Aggregate report pipeline** -- PBRER/DSUR/PADER status vs. calendar deadlines
5. **Risk management update** -- RMP/REMS action status, RMM effectiveness data
6. **Open CAPAs & deviations** -- PV quality system health (see 8-pv-quality)
7. **Vendor performance** -- any KPI breaches from Sub-process e above
8. **Regulatory intelligence** -- PRAC recommendations, FDA safety communications, MHRA alerts relevant to Prosinertimib or EGFR class
9. **Escalation items** -- issues requiring CMO attention (see 1-cmo, Sub-process 1)
10. **AOB**

### g. Benefit-Risk Assessment Leadership
Lead the periodic benefit-risk evaluation for Prosinertimib using a structured framework (FDA Benefit-Risk Framework or EMA PrOACT-URL). Integrate efficacy data from clinical development, cumulative safety data from ICSRs and clinical trials, and comparative data from the NSCLC treatment landscape. Update the benefit-risk assessment at each PSUR/PBRER cycle or whenever a validated signal materially changes the safety profile.

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] Structured benefit-risk assessment methodology.

### h. PV System Maintenance (PSMF, SOPs)
Review PSMF updates prepared by Manager, PV Quality & Compliance (8-pv-quality) and approved by QPPV (3-qppv), ensuring the PSMF accurately reflects the current PV system per GVP Module II. Approve PV SOPs maintained by 8-pv-quality and ensure they undergo periodic review (every 2 years minimum). Review training compliance reports from 8-pv-quality covering all PV personnel (internal and outsourced). Prepare for GVP inspections by reviewing the inspection-readiness checklist maintained by 8-pv-quality and updated quarterly.

**Ownership note:** Day-to-day PSMF maintenance, SOP lifecycle management, training tracking, and inspection readiness preparation are owned by 8-pv-quality. PSMF sign-off authority belongs to the QPPV (3-qppv). The Head of PS provides strategic oversight and final approval of SOPs.

## Escalation Path
- Reports to: CMO (1-cmo)
- Escalate immediately: fatal/life-threatening unexpected SUSARs, new signals suggesting increased mortality, any event requiring clinical hold, regulatory authority safety inquiries
- Escalate at next SMT: validated signals not requiring immediate action, aggregate report findings, vendor performance issues
