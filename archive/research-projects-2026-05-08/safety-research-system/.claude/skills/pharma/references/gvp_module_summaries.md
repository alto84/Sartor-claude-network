## <a name="module-i"></a>1. GVP Module I — Pharmacovigilance Systems and Their Quality Systems

**Reference:** EMA/541760/2011 | Effective: 02/07/2012
**Source:** [EMA GVP Module I](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/scientific-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-module-i-pharmacovigilance-systems-and-their-quality-systems_en.pdf)
**Legal basis:** Regulation (EC) No 726/2004 Art. 23; Directive 2001/83/EC Art. 104 (as amended by Regulation (EU) No 1235/2010 and Directive 2010/84/EU)

### 1.1 Qualified Person Responsible for Pharmacovigilance (QPPV)

**Location requirement:** The QPPV must reside and operate within the EU/EEA. (Dir. 2001/83/EC Art. 104(3)(a))

**Availability:** Must be continuously and permanently available to the MAH. Can be a direct employee or contractor, provided they remain "continuously at the disposal of" the MAH.

**Qualifications:**
- Appropriately qualified with documented experience in all aspects of pharmacovigilance
- Proficiency or access to resources in: medicine, pharmaceutical sciences, epidemiology, and biostatistics
- If the QPPV is not medically qualified, access to a medically qualified person must be available

**Core responsibilities:**
- Establish and maintain the MAH's pharmacovigilance system
- Have authority to influence the performance of the quality system and PV activities
- "Promote, maintain and improve compliance" with EU PV requirements
- Maintain awareness of product safety profiles and emerging safety concerns
- Act as single PV contact point for competent authorities on a 24/7 basis
- Ensure timely, complete, and accurate responses to authority requests
- Ensure quality, correctness, and completeness of PV data submitted
- Monitor ICSR submission compliance
- Determine RMP content and sign-off on PASS protocols
- Access Pharmacovigilance System Master File (PSMF) and safety database
- Receive and review audit schedules and quality system assessments

**Deputisation:** The QPPV may delegate specific tasks to qualified personnel, but overall responsibility for PV system oversight is non-transferable.

**Cross-functional interface:** The QPPV collaborates with management, safety/medical teams, medical writing, clinical study teams, regulatory affairs, case processing, QA/GVP compliance, and marketing functions.

### 1.2 PV System Quality Requirements

**Quality system components:**
- **Quality objectives** — Defined targets for PV system performance
- **Quality planning** — Documented plans for achieving quality objectives
- **Quality control** — Activities to fulfil quality requirements during task execution
- **Quality assurance** — Systematic activities to ensure PV outputs meet requirements
- **Quality improvement** — Continuous improvement based on CAPA findings
- **CAPA** — Corrective and preventive actions to address deviations

**Governance support requirements:**
- Structured handover procedures
- Staff training programs
- Regular PSMF updates
- Documented meeting minutes
- Compliance reporting mechanisms
- Database access controls
- Documented approval tracking for regulatory submissions

### 1.3 MAH Responsibilities for Outsourced Activities

The MAH retains full legal responsibility for all PV obligations regardless of outsourcing or delegation. Outsourced activities must be:
- Governed by written agreements defining roles and responsibilities
- Subject to oversight by the QPPV
- Included within the scope of the PV system quality framework
- Subject to audit and compliance monitoring

### 1.4 Organisational Impact

Module I mandates that every MAH must have:
- A named QPPV (EU/EEA-resident)
- A documented PV system with quality management
- Trained personnel with defined roles
- SOPs covering all PV activities
- Record management and document control systems
- Audit and inspection readiness

## <a name="module-ix"></a>2. GVP Module IX — Signal Management

**Reference:** EMA/827661/2011 Rev. 1 | Effective: 22/11/2017
**Source:** [EMA GVP Module IX Rev 1](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/scientific-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-gvp-module-ix-signal-management-rev-1_en.pdf)
**Addendum I:** [Methodological Aspects of Signal Detection](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/scientific-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-gvp-module-ix-addendum-i-methodological-aspects-signal-detection-spontaneous-reports-suspected-adverse-reactions_en.pdf)

### 2.1 Signal Management Process Steps

The process follows six sequential stages:

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **1. Signal Detection** | Identifying potentially causal drug-event associations using data from spontaneous reports (MAH database, EudraVigilance, FAERS, VigiBase), active surveillance, studies, and scientific literature. Combines manual ICSR review with statistical disproportionality analysis. |
| **2. Signal Validation** | Confirming a new potentially causal association, or a new aspect of a known association. Evaluates: (a) previous awareness, (b) strength of evidence (case numbers, data quality, consistency, biological plausibility), (c) clinical relevance including seriousness and vulnerable populations. |
| **3. Signal Confirmation** | Regulatory step where PRAC Rapporteur/Lead Member State confirms the signal within 30 days. |
| **4. Signal Analysis & Prioritisation** | Ranking confirmed signals based on impact on patients/public health and on the benefit-risk balance. |
| **5. Signal Assessment** | Comprehensive evaluation of all available pharmacological, non-clinical, and clinical data. PRAC may request additional data from MAH (typically 2-month deadline). |
| **6. Recommendation for Action** | PRAC issues recommendations: update product information, conduct studies, issue DHPCs, or other regulatory measures. |

### 2.2 EVDAS Screening Requirements

- MAHs must monitor EudraVigilance continuously and proportionately to risk
- **Minimum frequency: every 6 months** (recommended)
- Products on the additional monitoring list require more frequent screening
- More frequent monitoring (>every 2 weeks) may apply during public health emergencies
- Detection methodology combines manual ICSR review with disproportionality analysis plus clinical/scientific judgment
- "Not every statistical alert needs investigation, and some non-statistical findings do"

### 2.3 Signal Validation Criteria

Three core evaluation elements:
1. **Previous awareness** — Whether the association was already known
2. **Strength of evidence** — Case numbers, data quality, consistency, biological plausibility
3. **Clinical relevance** — Seriousness, impact on vulnerable populations

### 2.4 PRAC Notification Timelines

| Scenario | Timeline |
|----------|----------|
| **Emerging Safety Issue** (urgent public health impact) | **3 working days** — notify EMA and relevant NCAs |
| **Validated signal — important risk** (variation submission) | **3 months** |
| **Validated signal — other risk** (variation submission) | **6 months** |
| **Signal includable in upcoming PSUR** | Within **6 months** (if PSUR due in that window) |
| **Standalone signal notification** (if other options don't apply) | **30 days** |
| **PRAC Rapporteur signal confirmation** | **30 days** |

### 2.5 Roles in Signal Management

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **MAH** | Continuous signal detection from own database and EudraVigilance; signal validation; notification type decision; data provision on request |
| **PRAC** | Prioritises confirmed signals; appoints rapporteurs; requests additional data; issues recommendations |
| **NCA** | For nationally authorised products, the Lead Member State leads signal confirmation and assessment |

### 2.6 Organisational Impact

Module IX requires MAHs to maintain:
- Dedicated signal detection and management capability (personnel + systems)
- EudraVigilance access and monitoring processes
- Documented signal management SOPs with auditable tracking
- Medical/scientific expertise for clinical assessment of signals
- Capacity for rapid response (3 working days for emerging safety issues)

## <a name="module-v"></a>3. GVP Module V — Risk Management Systems

**Reference:** EMA/838713/2011 Rev. 2 | Effective: 31/03/2017
**Source:** [EMA GVP Module V Rev 2](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/scientific-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-module-v-risk-management-systems-rev-2_en.pdf)
**RMP Format Guidance:** [EU-RMP Template Rev 2.0.1](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/regulatory-procedural-guideline/guidance-format-risk-management-plan-rmp-eu-integrated-format-rev-201_en.pdf)

### 3.1 RMP Structure

The RMP consists of seven parts:

| Part | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Part I** | Product(s) overview — administrative information and product summary |
| **Part II** | Safety specification (subdivided into modules) |
| **Part III** | Pharmacovigilance plan |
| **Part IV** | Plans for post-authorisation efficacy studies |
| **Part V** | Risk minimisation measures |
| **Part VI** | Summary of the RMP |
| **Part VII** | Annexes |

### 3.2 Safety Specification (Part II)

The safety specification categorises safety concerns into three tiers:

**Important Identified Risks (IIRs):**
- Undesirable clinical outcomes with adequate scientific evidence of causal relationship
- Must be actively managed and monitored

**Important Potential Risks (IPRs):**
- Undesirable clinical outcomes where scientific evidence suggests possible causal relationship but is insufficient to confirm
- Require further characterisation

**Missing Information (MI):**
- Gaps in safety knowledge regarding specific anticipated uses or patient populations
- Under Rev 2: must be within approved indications and supported by scientific rationale
- Non-clinical safety findings summarised in module SII

### 3.3 Pharmacovigilance Plan (Part III)

**Routine PV activities** (beyond standard adverse reaction reporting and signal detection):
- Targeted follow-up questionnaires for special interest adverse reactions
- Enhanced passive surveillance systems
- Cumulative reviews of adverse events

**Additional PV activities:**
- Non-clinical studies
- Clinical trials or non-interventional studies
- Post-Authorisation Safety Studies (PASS)
- Studies evaluating risk minimisation measure effectiveness

### 3.4 Risk Minimisation Measures (Part V)

**Routine RMMs:**
- Summary of Product Characteristics (SmPC) recommendations
- Specific monitoring protocols (e.g., laboratory parameter assessments)
- Dose adjustments or treatment discontinuation procedures
- Contraception recommendations
- Packaging and labelling measures

**Additional RMMs** (essential for safe and effective use):
- Direct Healthcare Professional Communications (DHPCs)
- Educational programs for providers and patients
- Controlled access or distribution systems
- Pregnancy prevention programs
- Patient alert cards

### 3.5 When RMPs Are Required

- Initial marketing authorisation applications (mandatory for all new substances)
- Applications involving significant changes to existing marketing authorisations
- When requested by EMA or Member State authorities
- Post-authorisation: updates triggered by new safety information

### 3.6 RMP Lifecycle Management

RMPs are **dynamic documents** requiring continuous reassessment. Safety concerns may be removed or reclassified:
- **IIRs:** When fully characterised with no outstanding PV activities, or management integrated into standard clinical practice
- **IPRs:** When evidence strengthens (reclassify to IIR) or contradicts association (remove), or no further investigation possible
- **MI:** When adequate safety data becomes available

### 3.7 Organisational Impact

Module V requires MAHs to maintain:
- Risk management planning expertise (medical, epidemiological, regulatory)
- Cross-functional coordination for RMP development and updates
- Capacity to design and conduct PASS and effectiveness studies
- Educational material development and distribution capability
- Ongoing lifecycle management processes

## <a name="module-vi"></a>4. GVP Module VI — Collection, Management and Submission of Reports of Suspected Adverse Reactions

**Reference:** EMA/873138/2011 Rev. 2 | Effective: 22/11/2017
**Source:** [EMA GVP Module VI Rev 2](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/regulatory-procedural-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-gvp-module-vi-collection-management-submission-reports_en.pdf)
**Addendum I:** Duplicate management | **Addendum II:** Masking of personal data (effective 25/07/2025)

### 4.1 Valid ICSR Minimum Criteria

A valid Individual Case Safety Report requires four minimum elements:
1. An **identifiable reporter**
2. An **identifiable patient**
3. A **suspected medicinal product**
4. A **suspected adverse reaction**

All four elements are non-negotiable for submission eligibility.

### 4.2 Reporting Timelines

| Case Type | Timeline | Clock Start |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Serious ICSRs** (spontaneous) | **15 calendar days** | From Day 0 (receipt of minimum valid information) |
| **Non-serious ICSRs** (EU) | **90 calendar days** | From Day 0 |
| **Serious ICSRs from studies** | **15 calendar days** | From awareness by the MAH |
| **Follow-up information (serious)** | **15 calendar days** | From receipt of follow-up |

### 4.3 Medical Review Requirements

- All ICSRs require medical/scientific assessment
- SOPs and trained staff must be in place
- Data must be handled confidentially, accurately, and with full audit trails
- Seriousness documented at case level (E2B(R2)) or per reaction (E2B(R3))
- If QPPV is not medically qualified, access to medically qualified personnel is required

### 4.4 Causality Assessment

- Assessment of suspected causal relationship between medicinal product and adverse reaction
- Distinguished from "adverse events" (broader category without implied causation)
- Degree of suspected relatedness documented per ICH-E2B for each reported drug-event combination
- Multiple primary source assessments may be recorded

### 4.5 Follow-up Requirements

- Mandatory pursuit of additional information for incomplete reports
- Priority follow-up for: serious cases, pregnancy reports, cases of special interest
- Follow-up information for serious cases must be submitted within 15 calendar days of receipt

### 4.6 E2B(R3) and EudraVigilance Requirements

- ICSRs submitted electronically as structured data using controlled vocabularies
- E2B(R3) format enables more granular documentation including per-reaction seriousness
- Submission via EudraVigilance database (mandatory)
- MedDRA coding required for adverse reaction terms

### 4.7 Literature Monitoring

- MAHs must systematically monitor medical literature for case reports of suspected adverse reactions
- Specific guidance in GVP Module VI Appendix 2
- Worldwide literature monitoring required for MAH products

### 4.8 Consumer/Patient Reporting

- Unsolicited reports from consumers/patients are a valid collection source
- Reports must be processed through the same quality framework as HCP reports

### 4.9 Quality Management of ICSRs

- Quality system with SOPs and trained staff mandatory
- Data accuracy, confidentiality, and complete audit trails required
- Data quality monitoring processes (Appendix 6)
- Duplicate detection and management (Addendum I)

### 4.10 Organisational Impact

Module VI requires MAHs to maintain:
- 24/7 case intake capability
- Trained case processing personnel with medical review access
- Safety database with E2B(R3) export capability
- EudraVigilance submission infrastructure
- Literature monitoring resources
- Quality management system for case processing
- Capacity to meet 15-day timelines for serious cases

## <a name="module-vii"></a>5. GVP Module VII — Periodic Safety Update Reports

**Reference:** EMA/816292/2011 Rev. 1 | Effective: 13/12/2013
**Source:** [EMA GVP Module VII Rev 1](https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/scientific-guideline/guideline-good-pharmacovigilance-practices-gvp-module-vii-periodic-safety-update-report_en.pdf)
**Legal basis:** Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 520/2012

### 5.1 PSUR/PBRER Structure

The EU PSUR format aligns with the ICH-E2C(R2) Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report (PBRER). PSUR sections are described in GVP VII.B.5.1 through VII.B.5.21 and include:

- Executive summary
- Introduction
- Worldwide marketing authorisation status
- Actions taken for safety reasons
- Changes to reference safety information
- Estimated exposure and use patterns
- Data in summary tabulations (clinical trials and post-marketing)
- Summaries of significant findings from clinical trials
- Findings from non-interventional studies
- Information from other clinical trials and sources
- Non-clinical data
- Literature
- Other periodic reports
- Lack of efficacy in controlled clinical trials
- Late-breaking information
- Overview and conclusions on benefit-risk
- Signal and risk evaluation
- Benefit evaluation
- Integrated benefit-risk analysis
- Conclusions and actions
- Appendices

### 5.2 EURD List

- Comprehensive published list of active substances and combinations
- Contains: EU reference date, submission frequency, and Data Lock Point (DLP)
- Maintained by CHMP/CMDh after PRAC consultation
- Updated monthly; changes effective **6 months after publication**
- Available on EMA website

### 5.3 Submission Timelines

| Report Period | Submission Deadline (from DLP) |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Covering ≤12 months | **70 days** |
| Covering >12 months | **90 days** |
| Ad hoc (authority request) | As specified by authority |

### 5.4 Data Lock Points (DLPs)

- The DLP is the cut-off date for data to be included in a PSUR
- Determined by the EURD list based on the EU reference date and submission frequency
- DLP expressed in YYYYMM format in eCTD delivery files

### 5.5 Single PSUR Requirement

MAHs must prepare a single PSUR for all medicinal products containing the same active substance, covering:
- All authorised indications
- All routes of administration
- All dosage forms and dosing regimens
- Regardless of different product names or separate authorisation procedures

Unless otherwise specifically requested by competent authorities.

### 5.6 Benefit-Risk Evaluation

- Comprehensive and critical analysis of the benefit-risk balance
- Must incorporate new or emerging safety information in context of cumulative data
- Integrated evaluation across all available evidence sources
- Must address signals identified during the reporting period

### 5.7 PSUR Repository and Submission

- **Mandatory** since 13 June 2016 for all EU-authorised medicines (central and national)
- Submission via eSubmission Gateway/Web Client in XML delivery file format
- Direct national authority submissions no longer accepted
- IRIS platform (from January 2025) manages submission records

### 5.8 PSUR Single Assessment (PSUSA) Procedure

| Day | Milestone |
|-----|-----------|
| Day 0 | Procedure start |
| Day 60 | PRAC Rapporteur preliminary assessment report |
| Day 90 | MAH comments deadline |
| Day 120 | PRAC recommendation adoption |
| Day 134 | CHMP opinion / CMDh position |

### 5.9 Exemptions from Routine PSUR Submission

Products not requiring routine PSURs (unless EURD list specifies or authority requests):
- Generics (Article 10(1) authorisation)
- Well-established substances (Article 10a)
- Homeopathic products
- Traditional herbal products

### 5.10 Organisational Impact

Module VII requires MAHs to maintain:
- Medical writing and aggregate report authoring capability
- Benefit-risk evaluation expertise
- EURD list tracking and DLP management processes
- PSUR repository submission infrastructure
- Cross-functional data collection (clinical, post-marketing, non-clinical, literature)
- Capacity to meet 70/90-day submission deadlines
- Ability to respond within PSUSA assessment timelines

## <a name="cross-module-implications"></a>6. Cross-Module Implications for PV Org Structure

### 6.1 Required Roles (Derived from GVP Modules)

| Role | Primary Module(s) | Key Requirements |
|------|-------------------|------------------|
| **QPPV** | Module I | EU/EEA-resident, 24/7 available, authority over PV system, non-delegable oversight |
| **Deputy QPPV** | Module I | Qualified deputy for continuity; QPPV retains responsibility |
| **Case Processing Staff** | Module VI | Trained in ICSR handling, E2B(R3), MedDRA coding, 15-day timeline compliance |
| **Medical Reviewers** | Modules I, VI, IX | Medical assessment of ICSRs, signal validation, causality assessment |
| **Signal Management Analysts** | Module IX | EudraVigilance monitoring, disproportionality analysis, signal detection SOPs |
| **Aggregate Report Authors** | Module VII | PSUR/PBRER writing, benefit-risk evaluation, data integration |
| **Risk Management Specialists** | Module V | RMP development, PASS design, RMM effectiveness evaluation |
| **PV Quality/Compliance** | Module I | QMS oversight, CAPA management, SOP governance, audit readiness |
| **Literature Monitoring** | Module VI | Systematic worldwide literature screening |
| **Database/IT Support** | Modules I, VI | Safety database management, E2B(R3) compliance, EudraVigilance connectivity |

### 6.2 Critical Timelines Summary

| Obligation | Timeline | Module |
|------------|----------|--------|
| Serious ICSR submission | 15 calendar days | VI |
| Non-serious ICSR submission | 90 calendar days | VI |
| Emerging safety issue notification | 3 working days | IX |
| Standalone signal notification | 30 days | IX |
| Variation for important risk | 3 months | IX |
| Variation for other risk | 6 months | IX |
| PSUR submission (≤12 months) | 70 days from DLP | VII |
| PSUR submission (>12 months) | 90 days from DLP | VII |
| PRAC Rapporteur signal confirmation | 30 days | IX |

### 6.3 Quality System Requirements Across Modules

All PV activities must operate within a quality management framework (Module I) that includes:
- Documented SOPs for each activity
- Trained and qualified personnel
- Record management with audit trails
- CAPA processes
- Regular compliance monitoring and internal audit
- PSMF maintenance (see also GVP Module II)
