# 1. Chief Medical Officer -- Safety Oversight
> Parent: Board/CEO | Children: 2-head-ps | Cross-ref: 3-qppv (independence assurance)

## Role

The Chief Medical Officer (CMO) holds ultimate accountability for patient safety within the organization, second only to the CEO/Board. The CMO provides strategic medical oversight of all pharmacovigilance activities, chairs the highest-level safety governance body (Safety Review Committee), and serves as the final decision-maker for benefit-risk determinations that affect patients. The CMO ensures the Patient Safety function operates with adequate independence, resources, and regulatory compliance per ICH E2E, GVP Module I, and 21 CFR 314.80/312.32.

**Regulatory grounding:** ICH E2E Sections II-IV (Safety Specification, PV Plan, Risk Minimization), GVP Module I Section I.B.1 (QPPV authority), GVP Module II (PSMF/QPPV), CIOMS VI (Clinical Trial Safety Management), FDA Postmarketing Safety Reporting Guidance (2001).

**Escalation path:** CMO escalates to CEO and Board of Directors for safety issues with material business impact (e.g., product withdrawal consideration, Dear Healthcare Professional Communications, FDA Warning Letters).

## Sub-Processes

### 1. Safety Escalation Management

The CMO receives escalated safety issues from the Head of Patient Safety and QPPV that exceed the authority or scope of the Patient Safety function alone. Upon receiving an escalation, evaluate the clinical significance, regulatory urgency, and patient impact within 24 hours. Determine whether the issue requires immediate regulatory notification (e.g., urgent safety restriction per GVP Module VI), cross-functional mobilization, or Board notification. Document all escalation decisions with rationale in the Safety Review Committee minutes or, for urgent matters, in a CMO Safety Decision Memo.

**Key decision criteria:** (a) Is there an imminent risk to patient safety requiring immediate action? (b) Does this change the benefit-risk balance of the product (per ICH E2E Section IV risk characterization)? (c) Is regulatory authority notification required within a defined timeline (emerging safety issue: 3 working days per GVP Module IX Section IX.III; serious ICSR: 15 days per GVP Module VI; 7-day fatal/life-threatening SUSAR per 21 CFR 312.32)?

**Escalation decision thresholds:**
- Escalate to CEO/Board: any consideration of product withdrawal, clinical hold, or DHPC issuance
- Escalate to regulators: per timelines above; coordinate with QPPV for EU (see 3-qppv, Sub-process 4) and Regulatory Affairs for FDA
- Escalate to SRC (ad hoc): any validated signal classified HIGH priority (see 4-signal-mgmt, Sub-process d)

### 2. Benefit-Risk Decision-Making

The CMO is the final internal decision-maker on benefit-risk conclusions that will be communicated to regulators or reflected in product labeling. Convene the Benefit-Risk Evaluation Team (BRET) when a new safety signal, aggregate report finding, or regulatory query requires a formal benefit-risk reassessment. Ensure the assessment uses a structured framework (EMA PrOACT-URL or FDA Benefit-Risk Framework) and incorporates quantitative data (effect sizes, NNT/NNH, exposure-adjusted incidence rates) alongside qualitative clinical judgment. The CMO signs off on all benefit-risk conclusions in PSURs (ICH E2C(R2)), DSURs (ICH E2F), and RMP updates (GVP Module V).

**Output:** Documented benefit-risk position statement with supporting evidence, approved by CMO, filed in PSMF and shared with Regulatory Affairs for submission.

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] Structured benefit-risk assessment methodology skill with PrOACT-URL/BRAT framework implementation, effects table construction, and quantitative B-R scoring.

#### Benefit-Risk Assessment Framework (Merged from CMO Benefit-Risk Skill)

**Regulatory Grounding:**
- ICH E2C(R2) Sections 16-18: Benefit-Risk Evaluation in PSURs (Section 16 = Signal & Risk Evaluation, Section 17 = Benefit Evaluation, Section 18 = Integrated B-R Analysis)
- CIOMS IV: Benefit-Risk Balance for Marketed Drugs
- FDA Structured Benefit-Risk Framework (2018) -- five decision factors below
- EMA Benefit-Risk Methodology Project (PrOACT-URL)

Evaluates the overall benefit-risk profile based on available clinical data, preclinical evidence, and the known safety profile. Produces a structured assessment using the FDA's five Benefit-Risk Framework decision factors.

**Input:**
- Current efficacy data (response rates, duration of response, survival)
- Current safety data (AE rates by grade, SAE rates, deaths)
- Comparator data (standard of care outcomes)
- Population context (disease severity, unmet medical need)
- Risk mitigation measures in place

**Output -- Structured benefit-risk assessment using the FDA's five dimensions:**
1. **Analysis of Condition** -- disease severity, natural history, current treatment landscape
2. **Current Treatment Options** -- available alternatives, their benefits, risks, and limitations
3. **Benefit** -- clinical benefit evidence with effect sizes, NNT, uncertainty bounds
4. **Risk** -- safety profile summary with key risks, NNH, exposure-adjusted incidence rates
5. **Risk Management** -- mitigation strategies, REMS/RMP elements, monitoring plans

**Decision criteria for B-R conclusion:**
- Favorable: benefit magnitude exceeds risk in the context of disease severity and unmet need
- Unfavorable: escalate to CEO/Board; initiate regulatory notification planning (see 3-qppv, Sub-process 4)
- Uncertain: request additional data collection via PV Plan (ICH E2E Section III) or PASS (GVP Module V, Part III)

**Escalation:** Reports to CEO / Board. Escalate if benefit-risk becomes unfavorable or new safety signal changes profile.

**Dashboard:** Tab: Executive Summary -- extend with benefit-risk framework visualization. Widget: 2x3 grid showing the 6 FDA framework dimensions with traffic light status.

### 3. Regulatory Authority Interactions on Safety Matters

The CMO leads or participates in all high-level regulatory authority interactions concerning safety, including FDA Type A/B meetings with safety agenda items, EMA Article 20 referral procedures, PRAC rapporteur meetings, and PMDA safety consultations. Before any regulatory safety interaction, review the briefing document prepared by Head of Patient Safety and VP Regulatory Affairs. Ensure the company's safety position is scientifically defensible, internally consistent across regions, and aligned with the latest cumulative data. During interactions, the CMO serves as the medical voice of the company -- provide clear, evidence-based responses and avoid committing to actions beyond the agreed company position without post-meeting internal review.

**Preparation requirement:** Briefing document review at least 48 hours before the meeting. Pre-meeting alignment call with Head of PS, VP Regulatory, and Product Safety Physician.

### 4. Governance Chairing -- Safety Review Committee

The CMO chairs the Safety Review Committee (SRC), the highest cross-functional safety governance body. Before each monthly SRC meeting, review the agenda and pre-read materials prepared by the Head of PS, including: cumulative safety data summary, active signal status report, aggregate report pipeline, compliance KPI dashboard, and any escalated issues. During the meeting, ensure all safety topics receive adequate cross-functional input (PV, Regulatory, Clinical, Medical Affairs, Quality). After the meeting, review and approve minutes within 5 business days, ensure all action items have assigned owners and deadlines, and verify that escalation-worthy items are routed appropriately (to CEO/Board if needed, to regulators if needed).

**Standing SRC agenda items:** (1) Safety signal updates and dispositions, (2) Aggregate report status, (3) Compliance metrics (ICSR timeliness, SUSAR reporting), (4) Risk management effectiveness, (5) Cross-functional safety issues, (6) Regulatory intelligence.

### 5. QPPV Oversight and Independence Assurance

The CMO ensures the EU QPPV has adequate independence and authority per GVP Module I.B.1. Maintain a direct (dotted-line) reporting relationship with the QPPV so that the QPPV can escalate concerns about the PV system directly to the CMO without going through the Head of PS. At least annually, conduct a one-on-one review with the QPPV to assess: (a) whether the QPPV has sufficient resources and authority, (b) whether any concerns have been raised and adequately addressed, and (c) PSMF currency and inspection readiness. Document this review as evidence of management oversight per GVP Module I.

**Regulatory basis:** GVP Module I.B.1 requires that the QPPV has "sufficient authority to influence the performance of the quality system." The CMO is responsible for ensuring this authority is real, not nominal.

### 6. Clinical Trial Safety Oversight

The CMO provides medical oversight of safety monitoring across all active clinical trials. Review DSMB recommendations and determine company response within the timelines specified in the DSMB charter. Per ICH E2F Section 4, the CMO must ensure the Investigator's Brochure (IB) serves as the Reference Safety Information (RSI) for the DSUR and is updated when new safety information materially changes the known risk profile. IB safety updates must incorporate the latest aggregate safety data from both clinical trials and post-marketing experience (ICH E2F Section 18, CIOMS VI). The CMO reviews and approves the DSMB charter per ICH E2F, ensuring it defines: (a) DSMB composition and independence requirements, (b) meeting triggers (scheduled and event-driven), (c) unblinding rules (see 4-signal-mgmt, Sub-process i), and (d) stopping criteria with statistical boundaries.

For any SUSAR that meets criteria for expedited reporting (21 CFR 312.32: 7 calendar days fatal/life-threatening, 15 calendar days all others), verify that the clinical significance assessment by the Product Safety Physician is medically sound before distribution to investigators and regulators.

**Key accountability:** The CMO has final authority to halt a clinical trial on safety grounds, independent of commercial considerations. This authority must be documented in the company's clinical trial oversight SOP.

**Decision criteria for DSMB recommendation response:**
- Recommendation to continue: acknowledge and document within 5 business days
- Recommendation to modify (dose, eligibility, monitoring): convene internal review within 48 hours; implement or provide rationale for deviation within 10 business days
- Recommendation to pause/stop: implement immediately; notify QPPV within 24 hours (see 3-qppv, Sub-process 5); file clinical hold notification if applicable per 21 CFR 312.42

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] DSMB recommendation response framework and IB safety update process skill.

### 7. Safety Communication Approval

The CMO approves all external safety communications including: Dear Healthcare Professional Communications (DHPCs), patient safety alerts, press releases with safety content, and responses to media inquiries about product safety. Before approving any external safety communication, ensure: (a) the content is factually accurate and consistent with the approved label, (b) the communication has been reviewed by Legal and Regulatory Affairs, (c) the timing is coordinated with regulatory authority notifications (do not communicate externally before notifying regulators unless patient safety demands it), and (d) the tone is appropriately urgent without being alarmist.

**Regulatory basis:** GVP Module XV (Safety Communication), FDA Guidance on DHPC letters.

## Key Metrics the CMO Should Monitor

1. **ICSR compliance rate** -- % of cases submitted within regulatory timelines (target: >98%)
2. **SUSAR reporting compliance** -- 7-day and 15-day reporting timeliness (target: 100%)
3. **Signal-to-decision cycle time** -- days from signal validation to SMT/SRC disposition (target: <90 days)
4. **Aggregate report on-time submission** -- PSUR/DSUR/PBRER submission vs. due date (target: 100%)
5. **Open CAPA count and aging** -- PV quality system health indicator (target: 0 overdue CAPAs)
6. **QPPV escalation log** -- number and nature of QPPV escalations to CMO (monitor trend)
