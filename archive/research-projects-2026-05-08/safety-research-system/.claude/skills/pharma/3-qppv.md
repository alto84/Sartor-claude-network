# 3. QPPV (EU) -- Qualified Person for Pharmacovigilance
> Parent: 2-head-ps (solid line), 1-cmo (dotted line for independence) | Children: -- | Cross-ref: 1-cmo, 2-head-ps, 4-signal-mgmt, 5-pv-ops, 8-pv-quality

## Role
The EU QPPV is the legally designated individual responsible for the establishment and maintenance of the pharmacovigilance system per Directive 2010/84/EU and Regulation (EU) 1235/2010. The QPPV has regulatory-mandated independence and a dotted-line reporting relationship to the CMO, ensuring they can escalate PV system concerns without obstruction. For Prosinertimib, the QPPV oversees the PSMF, EudraVigilance compliance, and the LPPV network across all EU/EEA markets.

## Regulatory Grounding
- **Directive 2001/83/EC, Article 104(3)(a)** (as amended by Directive 2010/84/EU): QPPV must **reside and operate in the EU/EEA**. The QPPV shall be permanently and continuously available to the MAH.
- EU Regulation 1235/2010, Article 28a: QPPV responsibilities for centrally authorised products
- GVP Module I, Section I.B.1: QPPV must have "sufficient authority to influence the performance of the quality system"; non-delegable oversight responsibility
- GVP Module II: PSMF content, format, and QPPV oversight responsibility (see PSMF requirements below)
- EU Implementing Regulation 520/2012, Articles 2-7: PSMF content and format requirements

## Sub-Processes

### 1. PSMF Oversight & Maintenance
Ensure the Pharmacovigilance System Master File is current, accurate, and inspection-ready at all times per GVP Module II and Implementing Regulation 520/2012. Review PSMF within 30 days of any change to PV system structure (personnel, vendors, database, processes). Conduct full PSMF review annually. The PSMF must reflect the actual state of the PV system -- not an aspirational state.

**PSMF Required Content (per GVP Module II / Implementing Regulation 520/2012 Art. 2-7):**
- QPPV contact details and CV (Art. 2)
- Organisational structure of the MAH PV system (Art. 3)
- Data sources for PV activities (Art. 4)
- Computerised systems and databases used (Art. 5)
- PV processes (SOPs, work instructions) (Art. 6)
- Quality system documentation (audits, CAPAs, training records) (Art. 7)
- Contractual arrangements for outsourced PV activities
- List of all medicinal products covered by the PV system
- Logbook of PSMF changes

**Ownership note:** The QPPV has regulatory oversight and sign-off authority for the PSMF. Day-to-day PSMF maintenance (updates, version control, document management) is owned by the Manager, PV Quality & Compliance (8-pv-quality). The QPPV reviews and approves all PSMF changes before they become effective.

### 2. EudraVigilance Compliance
Oversee ICSR submissions to EudraVigilance via EVWEB/E2B(R3) gateway. Ensure compliance with EU expedited reporting timelines: 15 days for serious ICSRs, 90 days for non-serious. Monitor EudraVigilance rejection rates (target: <2%) and resolve systematic submission errors.

[NEEDS SUB-SKILL] EudraVigilance compliance monitoring and submission error resolution.

### 3. LPPV Network Management
Oversee the Local Person responsible for Pharmacovigilance (LPPV) network managed via PharmaLex service agreement. Ensure each LPPV understands local reporting requirements, has access to the safety database, and is trained on company SOPs. Conduct annual LPPV competency assessment.

### 4. Regulatory Authority Interface (EU/EEA)
Serve as primary PV contact for EMA, PRAC, and national competent authorities on PV system matters. Respond to QPPV-directed queries within regulatory timelines. Coordinate with Head of PS (2-head-ps) on safety referrals (Article 20/31 procedures).

### 5. Signal Management Oversight (EU)
Review and co-sign signal assessment reports before regulatory submission. Ensure signal management activities comply with GVP Module IX. The QPPV must have oversight of all validated signals and their regulatory implications for EU labeling.

### 6. Safety Communication Review
Review and approve EU-directed safety communications (DHPCs, safety variations, urgent safety restrictions). Ensure consistency with SmPC and PIL. Coordinate timing with national competent authorities.

### 7. PV System Quality Assurance
Monitor PV system performance indicators and escalate systemic issues to CMO (1-cmo). The QPPV has authority (per GVP Module I) to escalate concerns about PV system adequacy directly to senior management without going through the Head of PS reporting line.

## Deputisation Rules

Per GVP Module I, Section I.B.1, the QPPV may delegate specific tasks to qualified personnel, but overall responsibility for PV system oversight is non-transferable. Deputisation requirements:

- **Named deputy QPPV** must be designated and documented in the PSMF at all times
- Deputy must meet the same qualification requirements as the QPPV (Directive 2001/83/EC Art. 104(3)(a)): appropriately qualified, documented PV experience, EU/EEA resident
- Deputy must be trained on all products covered by the PV system and have access to the safety database and PSMF
- **Handover procedure** must be documented in an SOP covering: (a) planned absences (holiday, conference) -- deputy activated with written notification to PV team, (b) unplanned absences -- deputy activated immediately, CMO notified within 24 hours (see 1-cmo, Sub-process 5)
- **Scope of delegation:** The QPPV may delegate day-to-day tasks (e.g., ICSR review, PSMF updates) but must retain personal oversight of: signal management decisions (see 4-signal-mgmt, Sub-process e), PRAC notifications, urgent safety restrictions, and PV system adequacy assessments
- **24/7 availability:** Either QPPV or deputy must be contactable at all times per GVP Module I

## Key Metrics
- PSMF currency (days since last update vs. last system change)
- EudraVigilance submission compliance rate
- EudraVigilance rejection rate
- LPPV network coverage (% of marketed EU/EEA countries with active LPPV)
- QPPV escalation log (number and nature of escalations to CMO)

## Cross-Functional Dependencies
- Head of Patient Safety (2-head-ps): operational reporting line
- CMO (1-cmo): independence/escalation dotted-line
- Associate Director, PV Operations (5-pv-ops): EudraVigilance submission data
- Manager, PV Quality & Compliance (8-pv-quality): PSMF maintenance support, inspection readiness
- Regulatory Affairs: safety variation submissions, SmPC updates
- PharmaLex: LPPV service delivery

## Escalation
Reports to: Head of Patient Safety (2-head-ps, solid line), CMO (1-cmo, dotted line for independence)
Escalate to CMO if: PV system inadequacy identified, resource constraints affecting compliance, concerns about management interference with PV decisions
