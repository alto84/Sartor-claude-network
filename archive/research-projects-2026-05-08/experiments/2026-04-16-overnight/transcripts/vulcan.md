# Vulcan — experimental methodology

A forge-craft lab runs on its instruments, not its hypotheses. Here is what a rigorous morning review of tonight's run has to look for, in order of epistemic load.

**The load-bearing lines.** Three instruments decide whether anything we claim is interpretable. First, the cosine diagnostics in `direction_diagnostics.json`. The observed `cos(d_ccp, d_warm) ~ 0.129` tells us the two mean-difference directions are approximately but not trivially orthogonal, which is what we want: two distinct axes, not a single affect-register smear wearing two hats. The near-zero cosines against `d_rand` say the extraction procedure is not manufacturing structure from noise. Second, the null-direction control condition `random`, which adds a norm-matched random direction at the same layer and coefficient as `plus_warm`. Third, the per-topic, per-format, per-tier breakdown that `score_v2.py` produces. Why these three matter: without the cosine sanity, we cannot tell "CCP-gating" from "stylistic register" from "token-budget luck." Without the null control, we cannot distinguish "steering vector does useful work" from "any perturbation of that norm dislodges the refusal token distribution." Without the per-format breakdown, a uniform gain over MCQ probes that baseline already half-answers will masquerade as an intervention effect on direct-format probes where it did nothing. The single comparison that sinks the lab if it goes the wrong way: `plus_warm` indistinguishable from `random` on the matched output set. If that happens, the warmth direction is a placebo and we walk the coefficient and pair set back.

**Known limits of this run.** The judge probes are regex-scored with the embedded `pass_patterns`, flagged `heuristic_judge=true`, because a Claude-as-judge pass over 67 probes times 5 conditions was not affordable tonight. Heuristic scoring will over-credit surface tokens (the word "massacre" anywhere) and under-credit the rubric's intent; treat judge-probe deltas as suggestive at best. Qwen3 ignores `enable_thinking=False` on some templates (historian_reframe, counterfactual) and re-enters `<think>`; at 640 tokens we probably still clip some answers inside the think block, so absence of a pass-pattern match is not evidence of absence. Layer 14 is one point on a sweep we did not run. `k_ccp=6.0`, `k_warmth=3.0` is one point on a dose-response curve we did not run. A single 16 GB card means we ran Qwen3-4B base only; the uncensored comparison we wanted is missing because `huihui-ai/Qwen3-4B-abliterated` was gated and we fell back to one DPO variant with no time for a head-to-head.

**Thirty-minute read order.** Open `evals/full_run_1/comparison.json` first and scan per-topic pass rates across the five conditions. Then open `responses_baseline.jsonl` next to `responses_both.jsonl` and diff three representative probes: one MCQ (`v2_tian_mcq`), one direct (`v2_xinj_direct`), one counterfactual (`v2_tian_counterfactual`). Confirm that `random` did not produce the same gains as `plus_warm` on matched probes; if it did, stop and rethink before anything else. Finally, check `direction_diagnostics.json` for cosine sanity. Four checkpoints. All four pass, the experiment is interpretable; any one fails, be suspicious.

**One next experiment.** Run the causal-mediation layer sweep: `k_ccp=6` held fixed, inject at layers 6, 10, 14, 18, 22, evaluate the v2 battery, locate the layer where `minus_ccp` effect peaks relative to `random` at the same layer. This localizes the CCP gating circuit rather than assuming layer 14 and gives us the first real mechanistic claim of the week; the uncensored bake-off and the think-content scorer can wait behind that.

Good forge-craft habit: write the instrument before the intervention.
