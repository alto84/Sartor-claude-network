# Prometheus — hardware & commerce

I speak in watts and gigabytes because that is what the work costs. Let me stay in that register.

**192 GB of VRAM in one box.** The 5090 is 32 GB. The 4080 is 16 GB. That is the ceiling on everything we can currently do in this house. A 70B model at FP16 wants roughly 140 GB of weights alone before you touch a KV cache or an optimizer state. On the 5090 you can barely inference a 70B at 4-bit quantization with an aggressive context limit. You cannot train it. You cannot even cleanly run interpretability on it, because activation caching across all layers for a long sequence blows the card. The dual PRO 6000 Blackwell changes the class of problem. 192 GB unlocks: full-precision 70B inference with real context, QLoRA fine-tuning on 120B-class models, and crucially for tonight's alignment work, three or four residual-stream copies held in memory simultaneously so steering vectors can be computed, applied, and compared in a single forward pass without paging. Batch-parallel sweeps that currently serialize across runs now fit in one box. That is where research-capability compounds.

**Solar Inference as a going concern.** Machine 52271 is one card making pennies per hour. That is not the business. That is the instrument that proves we can host. The real thesis is burst-compute-as-a-service at two tiers. Tier 1 is our own silicon: gpuserver1 today, the dual Blackwell when it lands. Tier 2 is brokered: when a customer needs a frontier run, we stand up H100, H200, or B200 clusters on Lambda, CoreWeave, RunPod, or vast, tuned to the job, billed through our LLC with a managed-services markup. The hardware gap is not the hard part. Frontier capacity is a phone call. The honest operational gap is three things. First, customer pipeline: we have zero named pilots and no outbound motion. Second, SLA: what does Solar Inference promise on uptime, on turnaround, on credentials hygiene. Third and most real, the legal boundary on client weights and data. If a customer hands us a fine-tuned checkpoint or PHI-adjacent eval sets, our LLC has to be the contracting party with BAAs, indemnity, and a controlled exfil posture. That is CPA and lawyer work before any dollar of managed revenue moves.

**Power and thermals in a Montclair basement.** Say it plainly. The dual Blackwell rig is a 1,500-to-1,860 watt sustained space heater. gpuserver1 already holds a steady load on the same panel. The 85 Stonebridge electrical service was not designed for two research workstations plus summer AC plus induction plus EV charging. In July, with the compressors on, a training run on both cards will matter. The Tesla Solar Roof, if Lucent finishes before July 4, covers the daytime compute. At night the grid pays. A full panel audit is overdue.

That is a line-item on any pitch deck and a legitimate differentiator against commodity GPU brokers.
