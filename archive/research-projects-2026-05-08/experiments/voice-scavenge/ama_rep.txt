Letter for intent for AMA representative position:


My name is Emmett Sartor, though I usually go by Alton, my middle name.  I am interested in becoming the T1 AMA representative for Tulane.  I have always maintained a strong interest in national economics and politics, and now that I am (finally) in medical school, my interested have shifted to the economics associated with medicine.  I feel that as an AMA rep, I will have the opportunity to both advance the agenda of the medical profession, as well as learn how to better serve the medical community. 


In terms of my qualifications, I currently run an economics and politics group where national issues are discussed.  The current goal of these meetings is to open discussion hot political topics and provide a place for people to learn and voice their opinions on current topics.  I also have experience with leadership positions.  During undergrad, I was a Senior Reactor Operator for the Reed College research reactor.  In this position, I was responsible for a variety of important tasks, including training and emergency management.  I also had extensive legislative experience.  I have had the opportunity to debate mock legislation at the Louisiana State Capital in Baton Rouge, including the successful passage of a resolution to ban smoking in Louisiana restaurants.  This mock resolution in 2003 was prescient of legislation that was only recently considered in the current state legislature.  I hope that by becoming an AMA representative, I can influence real legislation that will address the concerns of the AMA.


Sincerely,
Emmett Alton Sartor
